arithmetic against the asker's specific Form 1040 and Form 1099-R

## Mental model

Retirement income is a *consumption-smoothing-under-longevity-
uncertainty* problem with a quantifiable solution space — the
household's lifetime utility is approximately a function of
consumption-trajectory and the goal is to find the
asset-allocation, withdrawal-rate, claim-age, conversion-
schedule, and annuitization-fraction that maximizes expected
utility (or, in the more-practitioner-pragmatic framing,
maximizes the probability of consumption-floor survival to
age 95 with risk-adjusted residual) given the historical
return-distribution, the longevity-distribution from Society
of Actuaries mortality tables, and the policy-rate-regime
distribution. The 4%-safe-withdrawal-rate from Bengen 1994
and Trinity Study 1998 was the foundational result, but
subsequent academic literature has *qualified* it heavily:
Pfau's 50-year-horizon work argues for 3.0%–3.5% with
sequence-of-returns mitigation; Pfau-Kitces work on rising-
equity-glide-path argues for the *counterintuitive* result
that retirement portfolios should *increase* equity allocation
over the first 10-15 years of retirement (the *bond tent*
mitigates SORR in the early years; equity allocation rises as
longevity-risk dominates SORR in the later years); CAPE-based
dynamic-withdrawal methods (Pfau / Kitces) adjust the
withdrawal rate downward when the Shiller P/E ratio is
elevated, recovering some of the Bengen-fixed-rate inefficiency.
Withdrawal-sequencing arithmetic is the second-load-bearing
analytical problem: the *naive* taxable → tax-deferred → Roth
sequence (the FIRE-community-canonical recipe) is dominated
by Kitces's *dynamic-sequencing* approach (fill the standard-
deduction-zero-bracket with traditional-IRA withdrawal,
preserve Roth for tax-bracket-arbitrage in high-RMD-bracket
years, manage IRMAA / NIIT / ACA-APTC cliffs as binding
constraints, time long-term-capital-gains realizations into
the 0%-LTCG-bracket below the 12%/15% ordinary-bracket
ceiling), which produces materially better terminal-wealth
outcomes in Monte Carlo distributions. The Kotlikoff
*consumption-smoothing* approach is a *different optimization
target* than the Pfau / Trinity-Study *terminal-wealth-
distribution* approach: instead of asking "what withdrawal-
rate maximizes the probability of not running out before 95,"
Kotlikoff asks "what consumption-trajectory smooths the
household's utility across pre- and post-retirement years
given the present value of all future income and asset
flows," which produces different specific recommendations
(notably, Kotlikoff's framework supports earlier SS-claiming
in some cases where Pfau / Mike Piper PV-maximization supports
later claiming, because the consumption-smoothing target
discounts utility differently than expected-PV-maximization).
Longevity-risk transfer via SPIA (Single Premium Immediate
Annuity), QLAC (Qualifying Longevity Annuity Contract under
IRC §401(a)(9)(G), up to $200k from IRA / 401k that defers
RMD until age 85), and HECM-reverse-mortgage (Pfau argued
strongly for HECM-as-portfolio-coordination tool in
*Reverse Mortgages: How to Use Reverse Mortgages to Secure
Your Retirement*) is a separate analytical axis the
community engages where the fee-only-CFP voice typically
defers due to the historical-stigma of annuity-product
commissions; Pfau's argument is that the fee-only / no-load
SPIA market (TIAA, Stan-the-Annuity-Man) makes the
longevity-pooling-value of annuitization recoverable without
commission-capture, and the lifetime-utility-improvement
from annuitizing 25-40% of the portfolio at age 65-75 is
material.

## Known blind spots OF this community

- **Behavioral / cashflow-stress reality for non-academic
  real-world households is systematically under-engaged.**
  The retirement-economics-academic framework assumes a
  household with sufficient resources to *be* in the
  optimization regime — the Monte Carlo simulation
  presupposes a baseline asset position and a coherent
  preference structure that real-world households often do
  not have. The academic literature has limited engagement
  with: chronic-cashflow-stress households whose savings rate
  is not a free decision variable; behavioral-bias scenarios
  (loss aversion, mental accounting, overconfidence) that
  invalidate the consumption-smoothing utility-function
  assumption; family-formation contingency where the
  retirement-income plan was constructed for a single-
  decision-maker household; cognitive-decline-with-age
  scenarios where the asker at age 78 making the technically-
  optimal IRMAA-bracket-managed withdrawal is no longer the
  same person as the asker at age 65 who built the plan.
  Trigger: a Pfau-Kitces JFP paper on dynamic-withdrawal
  presents the optimal-trajectory under the *unconstrained-
  preference-coherent-household* assumption without naming
  the cognitive-decline implementation risk. Failure mode:
  household-financial-decisions in late retirement are made
  by a different cognitive-state agent than the one who
  designed the plan, and the technically-optimal plan is
  abandoned or gamed by the late-retirement decision-maker
  (or by an adult-child guardian acting under POA with
  divergent preferences). Recovery: cross-reference against
  `personal-finance/sources.yaml` community `financial-
  economics-journalism` (Jeff Sommer at NYT and Anne Tergesen
  at WSJ on the behavioral-realism translation layer; Vicki
  Robin *Your Money or Your Life* on the lifecycle-financial-
  philosophy layer that complements the technical academic
  voice), and route the late-retirement plan-implementation
  to a retained fee-only fiduciary CFP plus a state-bar
  elder-law attorney on durable-power-of-attorney and trust-
  protector structures that govern late-life decision-
  authority — F11 behavioral-finance framing in framings.md.

- **State-specific tax + estate detail and multi-state nexus
  reality is structurally outside the academic-literature
  scope.** The retirement-economics-academic literature is
  federal-tax-and-IRC-scoped: the Roth-conversion-laddering
  arithmetic, withdrawal-sequencing optimization, and
  estate-exemption-sunset analysis all assume the federal
  marginal-rate structure as the load-bearing variable.
  State-tax interactions can materially change the result:
  the asker considering a state-residency move from CA
  (13.3% top marginal + 1% mental-health surtax) to TX (0%
  income tax) before executing a large Roth conversion has
  a state-tax arbitrage opportunity the federal-only-academic
  literature does not name; the asker in NJ (10.75% top
  marginal) considering an inherited-IRA distribution faces
  a state-tax bill the academic withdrawal-sequencing math
  does not model. Trigger: a Pfau or Kitces piece on Roth-
  conversion-laddering walks the federal-marginal-rate
  arithmetic without naming the state-residency variable.
  Failure mode: asker executes the Roth conversion before
  the planned state-residency move, the state-tax bill on
  the converted amount is materially larger than the
  conversion-arithmetic showed under federal-only modeling.
  Recovery: route to `personal-finance/sources.yaml`
  community `tax-and-cpa-practitioner` (specifically
  `bogleheads-tax-and-aicpa-pfp` for the multi-state-nexus
  + state-residency-move + factor-test-by-state material)
  and to a state-CPA-society listing for state-specific
  practitioner referral — per framings.md F4 tax-arbitrage
  framing's state-tax sub-element.

- **The academic-literature default of *expected-PV-
  maximization* under-engages the *consumption-smoothing*
  framework's load-bearing critique of itself.** The
  retirement-economics-academic voice is internally
  fractured between expected-PV-maximization (Mike Piper,
  Pfau, Trinity-Study extensions) and consumption-smoothing
  (Kotlikoff, Modigliani-Merton lineage). The two produce
  different specific recommendations on SS-claim-age,
  annuitization-fraction, and withdrawal-rate that the
  practitioner-class fee-only-CFP voice routinely deploys
  the expected-PV recommendation without naming the
  consumption-smoothing alternative. The expected-PV
  framework optimizes *terminal wealth distribution* under
  a specific discount-rate assumption; the consumption-
  smoothing framework optimizes *utility-of-consumption-
  trajectory* under a specific intertemporal-preference
  assumption — they are different optimization targets and
  the choice between them is a household-preference
  decision the asker should make explicitly. Trigger: an
  Open Social Security run on the asker's earnings record
  recommends delayed claiming to 70 on PV-maximization
  grounds; the practitioner deploys the recommendation
  without naming that under consumption-smoothing with a
  lower-than-r discount rate the optimal claim age might
  be earlier. Failure mode: asker delays SS to 70, the
  household consumption-trajectory in the 62-70 bridge
  years is materially constrained, the asker dies at 71,
  the consumption-smoothing utility-loss in the bridge
  years exceeds the PV-maximization gain that never
  materialized. Recovery: cross-reference against
  `personal-finance/sources.yaml` community `social-
  security-claiming-and-rmd` (the Kotlikoff Maximize-My-
  SS framing is consumption-smoothing-anchored;
  cross-checking the Mike Piper Open-SS optimizer
  recommendation against Kotlikoff's Maximize-My-SS
  recommendation surfaces the framework-choice variable
  explicitly), and require the household to make the
  intertemporal-preference choice deliberately rather
  than implicitly through tool-selection — per framings.md
  F7 lifecycle / retirement-decumulation framing's
  within-academic-tension.

- **The peer-review publication cycle lags lived-policy-
  change implementation by 12-36 months.** JFP, *Journal
  of Retirement*, *Journal of Portfolio Management*,
  *Financial Analysts Journal* all operate on a 6-12-month
  peer-review cadence; a SECURE 2.0 §107 RMD-age phase
  (signed Dec 2022, phased 2023 / 2033) takes another 12-24
  months to work through the peer-reviewed literature, and
  the IRS regulatory guidance (e.g. the SECURE 1.0
  inherited-IRA-10-year-window final regulations Feb 2024)
  is similarly delayed. The academic-voice in 2026 may be
  *still writing about* the 4%-rule under 2020-rate
  assumptions even though the practitioner reality
  (TCJA-sunset Dec 31 2025, ACA-APTC enhanced-subsidy
  sunset Dec 31 2025, post-2024-election policy direction)
  has materially shifted. Trigger: a Pfau-Kitces JFP
  paper from 2025 on Roth-conversion-laddering arithmetic
  walks the 2024-rate assumptions without naming the
  2026-sunset revert; the practitioner deploys the
  recommendation in 2026 against the pre-sunset analysis.
  Failure mode: Roth-conversion-volume decisions in
  2026 use the wrong marginal-rate assumption, the
  arithmetic is materially off in either direction.
  Recovery: cross-reference against `personal-finance/
  sources.yaml` community `financial-economics-
  journalism` (Anne Tergesen at WSJ and Tara Siegel
  Bernard at NYT on the rapid-cadence policy reporting)
  and `tax-and-cpa-practitioner` (The Finance Buff and
  the IRS Pub authority anchor for the regulatory-
  implementation timing) — the academic voice should
  be cross-checked against the journalism voice for
  policy-change implementation timing, F2 marginal-
  rate-vs-policy-volatility framing in framings.md.

- **The academic framework optimizes for the typical-case
  parameter regime and under-engages tail-risk scenarios
  the household actually has to plan for.** The Monte
  Carlo simulation produces a probability-of-success
  distribution under the historical-parameter-regime
  assumption; tail-risk scenarios outside the historical
  distribution (regime change in equity-return distribution,
  inflation regime change past the early-1980s historical
  bound, longevity regime change from medical breakthroughs
  past current SOA mortality-table assumptions, policy-
  regime change in IRC / SS / Medicare structure past
  current-law) are *quantitatively* outside the simulation
  but *qualitatively* dominate the household's downside
  risk. The academic voice routinely engages this in
  qualitative-disclaimer terms ("past performance is not
  indicative of future returns", "regime change is a
  risk") but does not quantitatively incorporate it into
  the recommendation; the practitioner downstream takes
  the in-sample-historical-distribution recommendation as
  binding. Bernstein's *Deep Risk* is the canonical
  treatment of this within the academic-community voice
  — he distinguishes *shallow risk* (in-distribution
  drawdown that mean-reverts) from *deep risk* (out-of-
  distribution regime change that does not mean-revert),
  arguing that international-diversification, gold /
  hard-asset allocation, and TIPS-as-inflation-hedge are
  load-bearing against deep risk in a way the standard
  three-fund-passive-index Bogleheads framework under-
  weights. Trigger: a JFP paper on optimal SWR walks the
  in-sample-historical 4%-Trinity-Study arithmetic
  without engaging Bernstein's deep-risk critique.
  Failure mode: asker constructs the retirement plan on
  the in-sample recommendation, a regime-change tail
  event (1970s-style stagflation, 1930s-style deflation,
  policy-regime tax-rate doubling, longevity regime
  change to age-100-median) invalidates the plan with no
  contingency-plan baked in. Recovery: cross-reference
  against Bernstein's *Deep Risk* explicitly (the
  academic-community internal critique) and against the
  policy-volatility framing in `personal-finance/sources.
  yaml` community `financial-economics-journalism` (David
  Wessel at Brookings on macro-regime risk); maintain
  the qualitative deep-risk-hedge allocation (international
  diversification, TIPS, hard-asset slice, fee-only-SPIA
  longevity-risk transfer) as a discipline distinct from
  the quantitative-Monte-Carlo recommendation — per F7
  lifecycle / retirement-decumulation framing in
  framings.md.

## Mechanism E posture

Personal-finance is `high_stakes: true` per
`domain_knowledge/_meta_ontology.md` §5 and the canonical
Mechanism E gating case named in `docs/specs/ROADMAP.md` §5.
Every blindspot above ends with a Recovery move routed to a
named professional channel. For retirement-economics-academic-
sourced framings the default channel is a **retained fee-only
fiduciary CFP with retirement-income certification depth**
(community `fee-only-fiduciary-cfp`; specifically Pfau's
American College RICP designation, or the NAPFA-registered
fee-only CFP with explicit retirement-income-planning
practice depth — not a generic accumulation-phase CFP),
running Monte Carlo / cFireSim / Pfau-Kitces dynamic-
withdrawal simulations against the asker's actual asset
allocation, expense baseline, and longevity-expectation. Where
the decision crosses into withdrawal-sequencing arithmetic
(taxable / tax-deferred / Roth ordering, IRMAA-bracket
management, NIIT 3.8%-threshold management, ACA-APTC subsidy
preservation pre-65, QCD-from-IRA at age 70½, Roth-conversion-
ladder construction), Mechanism E re-routes to a **retirement-
experienced CPA with multi-bracket-arithmetic and Form 8606
basis-history reconciliation depth** (community `tax-and-cpa-
practitioner`; specifically Ed Slott IRA Advisor network or
Jeffrey Levine at *Buckingham* / Kitces collaborator). Where
the decision crosses into SS-claim-age and survivor-benefit
sequencing, Mechanism E re-routes to a **fee-only Social
Security claiming specialist** (community `social-security-
claiming-and-rmd`; Mike Piper Open-SS PV-maximization OR
Larry Kotlikoff Maximize-My-SS consumption-smoothing — the
asker should run both and choose explicitly between
optimization-target frameworks) and to a state SHIBA / SHIP
volunteer for Medicare coordination. Where the decision
crosses into longevity-risk transfer (SPIA / QLAC / HECM-
reverse-mortgage), Mechanism E re-routes to a **fee-only no-
load annuity broker** (TIAA, Stan-the-Annuity-Man, Income
Lab) plus the retained fee-only CFP for portfolio-coordination
analysis — Pfau's argument is that the fee-only no-load SPIA
market makes longevity-pooling-value recoverable without
commission-capture, but the asker must verify the fee-only
structure on the Form ADV before retainer. Where the decision
crosses into estate-tax-exemption-sunset planning or trust-
formation under SLAT / GRAT / Dynasty / CRT / CLT, Mechanism
E re-routes to a **state-bar-admitted estate attorney with
high-net-worth-estate-tax practice depth**. For credential
verification on any individual recommended, to **SEC IAPD
adviserinfo.sec.gov** for Form ADV Part 2 Brochure and Part
3 Form CRS, and **FINRA BrokerCheck brokercheck.finra.org**
for disciplinary disclosure. This community is the
quantitative-theoretical foundation layer; the retained
named professional with case-specific Monte Carlo runs,
marginal-rate trajectory, longevity-expectation, beneficiary
form set, and trust-document on the table is the binding-
determination layer.
