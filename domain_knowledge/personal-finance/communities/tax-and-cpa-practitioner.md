## Mental model

Household financial decisions involving tax consequences must
be evaluated against the *specific statutory and regulatory
text that governs the transaction*, not against
practitioner-blog interpretations of that text, which may be
out-of-date or wrong. The analytical workflow is: (1) identify
the IRC section, Treas. Reg., Rev. Proc., Rev. Rul., Notice,
or IRS Pub that governs the transaction; (2) read the
statutory / regulatory text directly; (3) cross-check the
practitioner-blog interpretation against the underlying text
for accuracy and recency; (4) identify the specific tax form
(Form 8606 / Form 8889 / Form 8962 / Form 5329 / Form 5498 /
Form 1099-R / Schedule D / Schedule B / Form 8606 Part I & III
/ Form 8606 Part II) the transaction reports on; (5) verify
the mechanic against an actual completed-form example from a
prior year or from a practitioner-blog walk-through; (6) confirm
with a retained CPA before executing where the dollar amount or
irreversibility warrants. The IRS-publication-authority anchor
is load-bearing: when a practitioner-blog interpretation
diverges from IRS Pub text, the Pub wins absent a more-
authoritative Treas. Reg. or revenue-ruling that supersedes.
The Critic agent at runtime weights IRS-Pub citations heavily
on tax-mechanic claims; practitioner-blog citations are
secondary signal. The most-frequently-mishandled forms in
personal-finance per cross-community thread volume are: Form
8606 pro-rata rule on backdoor Roth (the asker has existing
pre-tax IRA balance, executes backdoor-Roth conversion, and
the IRS aggregates *all* traditional / SEP / SIMPLE IRA
balances across all accounts as of Dec 31 to compute the
pro-rata-taxable fraction — the asker who missed this rule
files an incorrect Form 8606 and faces back-taxes + interest
+ accuracy-related penalty on audit); Form 8889 HSA last-
month-rule recapture (the asker uses last-month-rule to
contribute the full annual limit despite partial-year HDHP
coverage, then fails to maintain HDHP coverage through the
13-month testing-period, triggering recapture of the prorated
excess with 10% penalty and inclusion in ordinary income);
Form 8962 ACA APTC reconciliation (the asker estimated MAGI
for the benchmark-Silver-plan subsidy, actual MAGI came in
higher, the asker owes back the excess APTC on the Form 8962
reconciliation, subject to the 400%-FPL cliff repayment
limitation that may or may not apply depending on the policy-
year regime); Mega-Backdoor Roth in-plan-Roth-rollover + after-
tax-to-Roth-IRA timing (the plan-document may or may not permit
in-service after-tax withdrawal; the asker who assumed it does
and contributes against the §415(c) total cap then discovers
the plan-document doesn't permit the in-plan-conversion or
in-service-distribution and the after-tax balance is locked in
the 401k until separation or rollover); Form 5329 missed-RMD
excise tax (the asker missed an RMD in year X; the SECURE 2.0
§302 reduces the excise tax from 50% to 25% (and to 10% if
corrected within the *correction window*), but the form must be
filed with a reasonable-cause statement for the IRS to waive).
Inherited-IRA 10-year-window mechanics under SECURE 1.0 final
regulations Feb 2024 are the load-bearing post-2024 trap: for a
non-eligible-designated-beneficiary inheriting from a decedent
who had reached RMD age (currently 73 / phasing to 75 under
SECURE 2.0 §107), the final regulations require *annual* RMDs
in years 1-9 of the 10-year window *plus* full distribution by
end of year 10 — not the previously-assumed-by-many flexible-
10-year distribution.

## Known blind spots OF this community

- **The IRS-Pub-authority anchor lags lived-regulatory-
  implementation by 6-18 months and state-specific tax
  interaction by indefinitely.** IRS publishes Pub 590-A /
  590-B / 523 / 525 / 550 / 969 / 974 on annual cadence
  (typically released Jan-Mar for the prior tax year), Rev.
  Procs. and Notices roll out as policy changes get
  implemented, and Treas. Regs. take 12-36 months from
  statutory enactment through proposed-rule + comment-period
  + final-rule process. The SECURE 1.0 enactment (Dec 2019)
  → final-regulations (Feb 2024) gap was a 4-year window
  during which the practitioner community was working from
  IRS Notice 2020-51 + the proposed-rule + community-
  consensus interpretation of the post-2019 inherited-IRA
  mechanics, and the Feb 2024 final regulations *changed*
  the consensus interpretation (requiring annual RMDs in
  years 1-9 of the 10-year window for certain beneficiary
  classes), invalidating planning that had been done in the
  interim. Additionally, the IRS publishes federal-only — the
  state-tax interaction (state-residency, state-source-
  income, state-Roth-conversion treatment, state-NIIT
  equivalents like NJ's 8.97% Net Investment Income, state-
  HSA deduction conformity or non-conformity like CA's
  non-conformity for HSA contributions) is entirely outside
  the IRS-Pub apparatus and requires state-CPA-society or
  state-revenue-department reference. Trigger: a Finance
  Buff post or Ed Slott IRA Advisor article cites the IRS
  Pub for the federal mechanic without naming the state-
  specific interaction the asker faces. Failure mode: asker
  in CA executes a backdoor-Roth based on federal-only
  mechanics, CA's non-conformity for HSA + complex state
  treatment of Roth conversions creates a state-tax bill
  the federal-only analysis did not predict. Recovery: route
  to a state-CPA-society listing for state-specific
  practitioner referral; for the IRS-Pub-versus-final-
  regulations timing question, cross-reference against
  *Tax Notes* + *The Tax Adviser* practitioner-monthly
  archives for the post-statute pre-final-regulations
  interim guidance — per framings.md F11 IRS-publication-
  authority framing and F4 tax-arbitrage framing's state-
  tax sub-element.

- **Tax-mechanic correctness does not imply household-
  decision correctness; the framework optimizes for
  filing-year-scoped tax minimization in a way that under-
  engages the multi-decade lifecycle-planning frame.** The
  tax-and-CPA-practitioner voice is *technically correct*
  on the form mechanics but routinely produces
  recommendations that minimize current-year tax at the
  expense of multi-decade lifecycle utility — the
  retirement-experienced CPA might recommend traditional
  401k over Roth on current-year-marginal-rate-savings
  grounds without engaging the post-RMD-bracket-compression
  arithmetic that the retirement-economics-academic voice
  models. Similarly, the CPA might recommend QCD over
  taxable-charitable-deduction on current-year-AGI-grounds
  without engaging the IRMAA-bracket-management or estate-
  basis-step-up implications that a retirement-income-
  certified fee-only CFP would model. Trigger: a *Finance
  Buff* post or Ed Slott IRA Advisor article walks the
  current-year tax-arithmetic for a Roth-vs-traditional
  decision without naming the multi-decade RMD-bracket
  arithmetic. Failure mode: asker chooses traditional in
  the 24% bracket pre-retirement based on current-year
  arithmetic; the same household's RMD in retirement falls
  in the 32% bracket because traditional-IRA balance grew
  for 30 years and forces RMD at age 73; the lifecycle
  arithmetic shows Roth would have been better even at the
  current-year contribution moment. Recovery: cross-
  reference against `personal-finance/sources.yaml`
  community `retirement-economics-academics` (Pfau / Kitces
  on lifecycle-Roth-conversion arithmetic) and `fee-only-
  fiduciary-cfp` (the retirement-income-certified CFP for
  the multi-decade lifecycle frame); the CPA's tax-mechanic
  recommendation should be cross-checked against the
  lifecycle frame before binding — per framings.md F2
  marginal-rate-vs-policy-volatility framing.

- **Practitioner-blog interpretation routinely runs ahead
  of (or behind) IRS guidance in ways the asker cannot
  detect without primary-source verification.** The Finance
  Buff, Ed Slott IRA Advisor, Jeffrey Levine on Kitces, and
  Bogleheads tax-forum threads are the canonical practitioner
  references — but the practitioner-blog community
  occasionally publishes interpretations that anticipate IRS
  guidance (which then arrives differently than anticipated)
  or repeats interpretations that have been superseded by
  later IRS guidance the blog hasn't updated for. The
  Notice 2023-62 SECURE 2.0 §603 Roth-catch-up
  administrative-relief delay was an instructive case: the
  practitioner-blog community had spent late-2022 / early-
  2023 explaining the §603 mandatory-Roth-catch-up for >$145k
  W-2 wages starting 2024; IRS Notice 2023-62 (Aug 2023)
  granted a 2-year administrative-relief delay to 2026; some
  practitioner blogs updated immediately, others kept the
  old advice live with no annotation. Trigger: an asker
  Googles "SECURE 2.0 Roth catch-up" in early 2024, lands
  on a pre-Notice-2023-62 practitioner-blog post that
  recommends executing the mandatory-Roth-catch-up election
  at the asker's plan, when the plan administrator is in
  fact still operating under the pre-2026 rules. Failure
  mode: confused election, plan-administrator rejects the
  request, asker's catch-up contribution for the year is
  mis-handled or delayed past Dec 31, the §415(c) total cap
  arithmetic doesn't line up. Recovery: verify any
  practitioner-blog interpretation against the underlying
  IRS Notice / Rev. Proc. / Rev. Rul. / final-regulation
  citation directly (IRS.gov Notices and Rev. Procs.
  archive), and against the *Tax Notes* + *The Tax Adviser*
  + *Journal of Accountancy* practitioner-monthly archives
  for the post-statute interim-guidance window — F11 IRS-
  publication-authority framing in framings.md.

- **The CPA community structurally under-serves equity-comp
  + ISO / AMT / 83(b) / QSBS §1202 / 10b5-1 mechanics
  unless the practitioner has specific equity-comp practice
  depth.** The retirement-experienced CPA community engages
  retirement-plan + IRA + RMD + Roth-conversion mechanics
  well; the equity-comp side (RSU vest taxation as ordinary-
  income on Form W-2, ISO bargain-element parallel-tax
  computation on Form 6251 AMT, 83(b)-election 90-day
  window on restricted-stock grants, QSBS §1202 100%-gain-
  exclusion 5-year holding period requirement, 10b5-1
  trading-plan timing for executive shareholder discipline)
  requires a *different* CPA practice depth that not every
  retirement-experienced CPA has. The Finance Buff, Ed
  Slott, and Bogleheads tax-forum threads engage equity-
  comp only intermittently and at the introductory level;
  the canonical equity-comp practitioner voice is more in
  the tech-career-domain community (see `domain_knowledge/
  tech-career/sources.yaml` community `equity-comp-cpa-
  and-securities-attorney`) and in *myStockOptions.com*
  (the Bruce Brumberg practitioner archive). Trigger: an
  asker with tech-employer ISO exercise + hold consults a
  retirement-experienced CPA who is not equity-comp-
  practice-deep; the CPA misses the AMT bargain-element
  parallel-tax computation, the asker faces an AMT bill
  at year-end with no corresponding cash inflow. Failure
  mode: asker borrows or sells other assets at
  disadvantageous timing to pay the AMT, plus loses the
  ISO-hold's qualifying-disposition long-term-capital-
  gain treatment if forced to sell post-AMT before the
  2-year-from-grant + 1-year-from-exercise holding period.
  Recovery: route equity-comp questions to an equity-comp-
  practice-deep CPA + securities attorney (per
  `domain_knowledge/tech-career/`) before any exercise-
  and-hold decision — F1 cross-domain edge in framings.md
  into tech-career.

- **The CPA community engages estate-planning mechanics
  intermittently and routinely defers trust-formation /
  beneficiary-form / 2025-doubled-exemption-sunset
  planning back to a state-bar estate attorney without
  named-attorney referral protocol.** The retirement-
  experienced CPA + Ed Slott + Natalie Choate community
  engages the *tax-mechanics* of inherited IRAs, basis-
  step-up at death, generation-skipping transfer tax, and
  RMD-orchestration for trusts-as-beneficiary scenarios —
  but the trust-formation drafting (SLAT, GRAT, Dynasty,
  CRT, CLT, ILIT) is unauthorized practice of law and the
  CPA cannot execute. The 2025 sunset of the doubled
  estate-tax exemption (currently $13.99M individual /
  $27.98M MAB, reverting to roughly half on Jan 1 2026
  absent Congressional action) creates a planning window
  the CPA can *flag* but cannot *execute*, and the
  practitioner-community-blog voice routinely defers to
  "consult your estate attorney" without naming the
  anti-clawback §20.2010-1(c) regulation that protects
  pre-sunset gifts from post-sunset clawback if the gift
  is made by Dec 31 2025, nor providing a state-bar
  referral protocol. Trigger: an asker with $8M-$15M of
  assets asks the CPA community about estate planning
  under the sunset; the CPA flags the planning window but
  defers without named-attorney referral; the asker
  Googles "estate attorney near me" and selects a general-
  practice attorney without high-net-worth-estate-tax
  practice depth. Failure mode: the general-practice
  attorney drafts a vanilla revocable-living-trust without
  engaging the anti-clawback opportunity; the sunset
  arrives Jan 1 2026; the doubled-exemption is gone; the
  household has paid attorney fees without capturing the
  planning leverage. Recovery: route to a state-bar-
  admitted estate attorney with explicit *high-net-worth-
  estate-tax* practice depth (American College of Trust
  and Estate Counsel ACTEC fellowship is the canonical
  credential signal; ABA *Real Property, Trust and Estate
  Law* section publications are the canonical
  practitioner-literature signal); the CPA coordinates the
  tax-mechanics; the attorney drafts and files — per
  framings.md F12 estate / intergenerational framing and
  the F10 selective-referral matrix.

## Mechanism E posture

Personal-finance is `high_stakes: true` per
`domain_knowledge/_meta_ontology.md` §5 and the canonical
Mechanism E gating case named in `docs/specs/ROADMAP.md` §5.
Every blindspot above ends with a Recovery move routed to a
named professional channel. For tax-and-CPA-practitioner-
sourced framings the default channel is a **retained
retirement-experienced CPA with AICPA PFP Section credential
or PFS designation** (community `tax-and-cpa-practitioner`),
filing the actual Form 1040 + Form 8606 + Form 8889 + Form
8962 + Form 5329 with the asker's specific basis history,
HSA testing-period status, ACA-APTC MAGI estimate-vs-actual,
and missed-RMD reasonable-cause statement. Where the decision
crosses into multi-state nexus or state-residency-move
arithmetic, Mechanism E re-routes to a **state-CPA-society
listing with the specific state-tax practice depth** (NYSSCPA,
CalCPA, etc.) plus the state-revenue-department's nexus and
factor-test publications. Where the decision crosses into
equity-comp (RSU / ISO / NSO / ESPP / 83(b) / QSBS §1202 /
10b5-1), Mechanism E re-routes to an **equity-comp-practice-
deep CPA + securities attorney** (per `domain_knowledge/
tech-career/sources.yaml`) — generic retirement-experienced
CPAs are not equity-comp-practice-deep absent explicit
disclosure. Where the decision crosses into estate-planning
(SLAT, GRAT, Dynasty, CRT, CLT, beneficiary-form audit,
2025-doubled-exemption-sunset anti-clawback planning),
Mechanism E re-routes to a **state-bar-admitted estate
attorney with high-net-worth-estate-tax practice depth**
(ACTEC fellowship credential signal). Where the decision
crosses into withdrawal-sequencing or lifecycle-Roth-
conversion-laddering, Mechanism E re-routes to a **retained
fee-only fiduciary CFP with retirement-income certification
depth** (community `fee-only-fiduciary-cfp`; Pfau's
American College RICP designation) — the CPA does the
current-year-tax-mechanic; the retirement-income-certified
CFP does the multi-decade lifecycle arithmetic. Where the
decision crosses into SS-claim-age + RMD-orchestration as
one unified problem, Mechanism E re-routes to a **fee-only
Social Security claiming specialist** (community `social-
security-claiming-and-rmd`; Mike Piper Open-SS or Larry
Kotlikoff Maximize-My-SS). Where the decision crosses into
401k-rollover plan-document interpretation or fiduciary-
breach analysis, Mechanism E re-routes to a **retained
ERISA attorney** (community `erisa-and-fiduciary-rule-
law`). For credential verification on any individual
recommended, to **SEC IAPD adviserinfo.sec.gov** and
**FINRA BrokerCheck brokercheck.finra.org**; for the CPA's
own credential, to the state-board-of-accountancy license
verification + AICPA member directory + PFS-designation
verification. This community is the tax-mechanic + IRS-Pub-
authority anchor layer; the retained named professional
with case-specific basis history, multi-state-nexus facts,
and current-year filing requirements on the table is the
binding-determination layer.
