# Fee-Only Fiduciary CFP

NAPFA / XY-Planning-Network / Garrett-Planning-Network fee-only
fiduciary CFP voice — the practitioners who hold themselves out as
fiduciary on the *full client relationship* (not just the IRA-rollover
slice scrutinized by the DOL Fiduciary Rule), accept *no commission or
12b-1 trail* from product sponsors, and disclose compensation as AUM-
fee / hourly / flat-fee / project / subscription rather than commission
or "fee-based" (the regulatory-arbitrage label that allows commission
income alongside advisory fees). This community is a SOURCE of
decision-support framings on brokerage allocation, retirement-income
sequencing, behavioral coaching, and the structural-conflict-of-interest
lens that distinguishes fiduciary from suitability standards; it is
**not a substitute** for case-specific advice from a retained
fee-only-fiduciary CFP with the asker's actual income, marginal-rate,
plan-document, and beneficiary-form facts in hand. The blog post,
forum thread, or Kitces *Nerd's Eye View* article tells you where the
analysis happens; the NAPFA-registered or XY-Planning fee-only CFP
under a fully-disclosed engagement is where the binding planning
conversation occurs with the asker's specific tax-loss-harvesting
positions, Form 8606 basis history, employer plan-document features,
and SS earnings-record on the table.

## Identity

Practicing fee-only CFP® professionals who maintain NAPFA membership
(highest fee-only floor — fee-only-by-definition, not "fee-based" or
"fee-and-commission"), XY-Planning-Network membership (fee-only +
monthly-subscription + Gen X / Gen Y target market), Garrett-Planning-
Network membership (fee-only + hourly-or-flat-fee, no AUM minimum,
democratizes access for non-asset-rich households), or AdvisoryHQ
fee-only-vetted listing. They reason about household financial
decisions as an *agency problem*: the asker is principal, the advisor
is agent, and the structural compensation arrangement determines
whether agent-incentives align with principal-outcomes. They are the
practitioners who, when an asker asks "should I roll my 401k to an
IRA," first answer "what are the comparative expense ratios, fund
selection, creditor-protection, RMD-aggregation, NUA-eligibility, and
in-plan-Roth-conversion features at the source plan versus the
proposed IRA — and what is *my* AUM-fee on the rolled balance going
to be," because they know the rolling-AUM-fee answer is the
load-bearing variable in whether the rollover serves the client or
serves the advisor's AUM. They are *not* the broker-dealer registered
representative (whose suitability-standard duty allows commission-
incentive recommendations), *not* the dually-registered hybrid advisor
(who flips fiduciary status off when commission-product opportunities
appear), and *not* the AUM-only practitioner who structurally won't
serve a $40k-portfolio asker who needs the planning more than the
million-dollar-portfolio asker does.

## Mental model

A household's long-term financial outcome is most exposed not to
asset selection (which efficient markets and low-cost index funds
mostly handle) but to *structural compensation arrangements* between
the household and any advisor in the relationship — the difference
between paying 0.30% on a flat fee for actual planning and paying
1.0%–1.5% AUM forever on a "wealth management" relationship that
embeds rebalancing, planning, and asset selection together
compounds to seven figures over a 30-year retirement. The fee-only
structure (no commission, no 12b-1, no soft-dollar) is the *minimum
necessary condition* for fiduciary alignment but is not by itself
sufficient — within fee-only, AUM-fee compensation still creates an
incentive to *grow AUM*, which means the AUM-fee fee-only advisor
has a structural bias *against* paying down a mortgage from
brokerage assets, *against* annuitizing into a SPIA / QLAC,
*against* gifting to charity via QCD or DAF, *against* recommending
a 401k → IRA rollover *not* happen, all of which reduce AUM the
advisor charges on. The CFP Board's *Standards of Conduct* (October
2019, expanded 2024) require fiduciary duty across the relationship,
but enforcement is private and disciplinary-action volume is small
relative to the population of CFPs; reliance on the credential alone
without verifying NAPFA membership, AUM-fee-vs-flat-fee structure,
and *Form ADV Part 2 Brochure* + *Part 3 Client Relationship
Summary* leaves the asker exposed to the regulatory-arbitrage
"fee-based" advisor who holds out as fiduciary but earns commission
on the variable annuity recommended into the rollover. Decision
structure favors planning-first / product-second: the planning
deliverable (cashflow projection, retirement-income glide-path,
Roth-conversion-ladder schedule, beneficiary-form audit) is the
work that justifies the fee; the product implementation (which
index ETF, which brokerage, which 529 plan) is downstream commodity
selection that should not absorb the bulk of the engagement hours.

## Known blind spots OF this community

- **AUM-fee fee-only advisors structurally under-weight de-
  accumulation decisions that reduce AUM.** Within "fee-only," the
  AUM-fee compensation model creates a persistent incentive against
  recommendations that shrink the fee base: paying off a 6% mortgage
  from $400k of brokerage assets, gifting $50k via QCD at age 73 to
  satisfy RMD, annuitizing $500k into a fee-only-no-load SPIA via
  Stan-the-Annuity-Man at 65 for longevity protection, rolling an
  inherited 401k into the *plan* rather than out to an IRA where the
  AUM fee would apply. Trigger: an asker with a 5%–7% mortgage,
  $500k+ in taxable brokerage, asks "should I pay off the mortgage,"
  and the AUM-fee CFP runs the analysis with assumed brokerage
  return at 7% nominal without modeling the *certain*-after-tax-IRR
  of mortgage payoff against the *uncertain*-pre-tax brokerage
  return after the AUM fee and tax drag. Failure mode: asker keeps
  the mortgage through retirement, the advisor's AUM stays inflated,
  the household's cashflow is more rigid than it had to be in the
  drawdown phase, and sequence-of-returns risk in early retirement
  is amplified. Recovery: route the question to a *flat-fee* or
  *hourly* fee-only-CFP via Garrett-Planning-Network whose
  compensation is structurally independent of which side the
  decision lands on, OR to a retirement-experienced CPA who'll
  model the after-tax-IRR comparison without an AUM stake — per
  `personal-finance/sources.yaml` community `tax-and-cpa-
  practitioner` and the framings.md F10 within-fee-only-variation
  blindspot.

- **The credential is not the protection — the engagement structure
  is.** CFP® designation alone is held by ~100,000 US professionals
  spanning fee-only, fee-based, commission-only, dually-registered,
  hybrid, captive, and bank-channel arrangements; the credential
  signals the holder passed the CFP exam and completes 30 hours of
  continuing education biannually, but does not constrain the
  compensation model. Fee-only CFPs writing for the community-blog
  audience routinely understate this and assume the asker will read
  the *Form ADV Part 2 Brochure* and *Form ADV Part 3 CRS* to
  distinguish — askers don't. Trigger: an asker reads a fee-only-
  CFP blog post on Roth conversions, walks into a "wealth management"
  conversation at their bank-affiliated broker-dealer where the
  advisor introduces as "CFP," and assumes the framing transfers.
  Failure mode: asker accepts a variable annuity recommendation
  inside the rollover because the "CFP" credential pattern-matched
  to the blog-author voice, paying 2.5%–4.0%/year in M&E + rider
  costs the blog community would never have endorsed. Recovery:
  before any retainer, verify on SEC IAPD adviserinfo.sec.gov (Form
  ADV Part 2A item 5 *Fees and Compensation* — fee-only firms
  affirmatively declare no commission income; "fee-based" firms
  declare both fee and commission revenue lines) and on FINRA
  BrokerCheck for disciplinary disclosure — per `personal-finance/
  sources.yaml` community `erisa-and-fiduciary-rule-law` for the
  verification methodology and the F10 framing's third-bullet
  structural floor.

- **Behavioral-coaching capacity varies wildly within fee-only — and
  the blog voice often skews toward the technically-optimized asker.**
  The published fee-only-CFP voice on Kitces and *Journal of Financial
  Planning* presupposes an asker who has *already done* the
  behavioral work of saving 15%–25% of income, maintaining 3-6
  months of emergency fund, paying off credit-card debt; the
  technically-optimal Roth-conversion-ladder or asset-location
  analysis is the entire content. For an asker still in the
  behavioral pre-work phase (credit-card-debt-cycle, intermittent
  savings, lifestyle-inflation pressure), the technically-optimal
  advice can be actively counterproductive — recommending "max your
  401k to 23.5k" to an asker who'll then run up credit-card debt
  on the cashflow shortfall produces worse outcomes than "automate
  $400/month into Roth IRA and pay down cards first." Trigger: an
  asker carrying $15k of credit-card debt asks the fee-only CFP
  community about Roth-vs-traditional 401k, and the framing
  reflexively answers the tax-arbitrage question without naming
  that the behavioral-pre-work question is dominant at this stage.
  Failure mode: asker maxes 401k traditional on advice, the
  cashflow shortfall recurs as credit-card debt at 22% APR,
  net-worth trajectory negative despite "optimized" tax treatment.
  Recovery: cross-reference against `personal-finance/sources.yaml`
  community `bogleheads-and-passive-index` (the r/personalfinance
  flowchart's explicit *step 2: pay down debt above 7% APR
  dominates* sequencing) and against `personal-finance-influencer
  / mass-media voice` (Ramit Sethi's behavioral-systems framing
  and Dave Ramsey's *baby steps* for the behavioral-pre-work
  layer) — the fee-only-CFP technical-optimization framing applies
  *after* the behavioral baseline is in place; this is the F11
  behavioral-finance vs F1 priority-order tension flagged in
  framings.md.

- **Estate-planning coordination is structurally outside scope.**
  Fee-only CFPs operating under CFP Board Standards do not draft
  trust documents, wills, powers of attorney, healthcare proxies,
  or beneficiary designations that bypass probate via TOD / POD —
  this is unauthorized practice of law in 50 of 50 states, and the
  CFP whose scope is "comprehensive financial planning" still has
  to *route* the estate work to a state-bar-admitted estate
  attorney. The fee-only-CFP voice routinely names the topic
  ("you should have a will, a healthcare proxy, beneficiary forms
  current"), but does not engage the actual drafting choice
  (SLAT vs DAPT, GRAT-zeroing-out at IRC §7520-rate, dynasty-trust
  state selection between SD / NV / DE / AK / TN). The 2025 sunset
  of the doubled estate-tax exemption (currently $13.99M individual
  / $27.98M MAB, reverting roughly to half on Jan 1 2026 absent
  Congressional action) drives a planning window the fee-only CFP
  can *flag* but cannot *execute* without estate-attorney
  collaboration. Trigger: an asker with $8M–$15M of assets asks
  the CFP community about estate planning under the sunset, and
  the framing reflexively defers to "consult your estate attorney"
  without naming the *anti-clawback* §20.2010-1(c) regulation that
  protects pre-sunset gifts from post-sunset clawback if the gift
  is made by Dec 31 2025. Failure mode: asker reads the blog,
  defers the estate-attorney appointment past Q1 2026, the doubled
  exemption sunsets, the gift opportunity closes. Recovery: route
  the question to a state-bar-admitted estate attorney with
  high-net-worth-estate-tax practice depth — per `personal-finance/
  sources.yaml` cross-listed reference and the F10 selective-
  referral matrix in framings.md; the CFP coordinates; the attorney
  drafts and files.

- **AUM-fee fee-only practitioners can structurally under-serve
  the under-$250k-portfolio asker.** Most NAPFA-registered AUM-fee
  firms operate with a $250k–$1M client minimum because the
  advisor's hour-economics don't work below that threshold at
  the typical 0.75%–1.0% AUM rate; the published "find a fee-only
  CFP" advice routinely understates the access gap for the
  middle-income / early-career / FIRE-pre-accumulation asker.
  XY-Planning-Network (monthly subscription, no AUM minimum) and
  Garrett-Planning-Network (hourly-or-flat-fee, no AUM minimum)
  were both founded explicitly to close this gap, but their
  membership is materially smaller than NAPFA's AUM-fee population,
  and the typical Google-search for "fee-only CFP near me" returns
  AUM-fee firms first. Trigger: an asker with $40k of investable
  assets reads a fee-only-CFP blog post, contacts a NAPFA firm,
  is turned away or quoted a $4k flat-fee for a one-time financial
  plan that they cannot afford. Failure mode: asker concludes that
  fee-only advice is "for rich people" and defaults to a no-load
  index-fund DIY strategy without the missing planning layer
  (beneficiary-form audit, employer-plan in-plan-Roth-conversion
  feature check, HSA-eligibility verification, ESPP-discount
  capture), losing more in missed-planning-leverage than the
  $4k fee would have cost. Recovery: route specifically to
  XY-Planning-Network (xyplanningnetwork.com find-an-advisor) or
  Garrett-Planning-Network (garrettplanningnetwork.com find-an-
  advisor) by structure rather than to "any fee-only CFP" — per
  `personal-finance/sources.yaml` community `fee-only-fiduciary-
  cfp` notes section, and F10 framing's within-fee-only-variation
  axis.

## Mechanism E posture

Personal-finance is `high_stakes: true` per
`domain_knowledge/_meta_ontology.md` §5 and the canonical
Mechanism E gating case named in
`docs/specs/ROADMAP.md` §5. Every blindspot above ends with a
Recovery move routed to a named professional channel. For
fee-only-fiduciary-CFP-sourced framings the default channel is a
**retained fee-only fiduciary CFP via NAPFA / XY-Planning-Network /
Garrett-Planning-Network** with the engagement structure (AUM-fee
vs hourly vs flat-fee vs subscription) selected to match the
decision-cadence — AUM-fee for ongoing portfolio management;
hourly or flat-fee for one-time questions (Roth-conversion-ladder
design, beneficiary-form audit, in-plan-Roth-conversion-feature
check); subscription for cadenced quarterly review without AUM
capture. Where the decision crosses into tax-arbitrage / NIIT /
multi-state / Form-8606-basis-history, Mechanism E re-routes the
channel to a **retirement-experienced CPA** (community
`tax-and-cpa-practitioner`); into SS-claim-age sequencing, to a
**fee-only Social Security claiming specialist** (community
`social-security-claiming-and-rmd`, Mike Piper Open-Social-Security
or Larry Kotlikoff Maximize-My-Social-Security, or the federally-
funded SHIBA / SHIP volunteer via shiphelp.org for Medicare
coordination); into estate-tax-exemption-sunset planning or trust
drafting, to a **state-bar-admitted estate attorney** with
high-net-worth practice depth; into 401k-rollover plan-document
interpretation or fiduciary-breach §404 / §405 claims, to a
**retained ERISA attorney** (community `erisa-and-fiduciary-rule-
law`); for credential verification on any individual recommended,
to **SEC IAPD adviserinfo.sec.gov** for the Form ADV Part 2
Brochure and Part 3 Form CRS, and to **FINRA BrokerCheck
brokercheck.finra.org** for the disciplinary-disclosure record.
This community is the framing layer; the retained named
professional with case-specific income, Form 8606 basis history,
employer plan-document, beneficiary form set, and SS earnings
record on the table is the binding-determination layer.
