attorney), *not* the retirement-experienced CPA (whose practice
is tax-mechanic-scoped and does not engage fiduciary-breach
litigation), *not* the broker-dealer registered representative
(whose duty is suitability-standard not fiduciary, and whose
practice the ERISA / fiduciary-law community routinely scrutinizes
in litigation), and *not* the state-bar estate attorney (whose
practice is trust-formation / will-drafting / probate scoped,
though there is meaningful overlap on beneficiary-designation
audit and QDRO drafting).

## Mental model

Household financial decisions involving ERISA-qualified retirement
plans (employer 401k, 403b, 457, defined-benefit pension, ESOP,
SIMPLE-IRA, SEP-IRA at certain plan-sponsor configurations) are
governed by the *statutory-fiduciary-duty framework of ERISA Title
I*, with the plan-sponsor / plan-administrator / plan-investment-
manager / investment-adviser-providing-fiduciary-advice all
subject to ERISA §404's *duty of loyalty + duty of prudence + duty
to follow plan documents + duty to diversify investments*, and
the plan-participant / plan-beneficiary protected by ERISA §502(a)
civil enforcement plus the DOL EBSA administrative-enforcement
apparatus. The *load-bearing practitioner-concern* is the
401k → IRA rollover-recommendation: when an asker separates from
employment with a 401k balance, the standard advisor-recommendation
is "roll your 401k to an IRA at our firm" — but the IRA-rollover
benefits the *advisor's AUM* (the new IRA balance is fee-
generating) more than the *client's outcome* (the source 401k
plan may have lower expense ratios, better fund-selection, better
creditor-protection under ERISA §1144 + *Patterson v Shumate*,
NUA-eligibility on employer-stock, in-plan-Roth-conversion
features, and aggregation-with-other-employer-balances for RMD
calculation). The DOL Retirement Security Rule + PTE 2020-02 (2024
final) require *documented suitability analysis* of any rollover-
recommendation, including comparison of source-plan vs proposed-
IRA on expense ratios, fund selection, services, fees, and
features; the practitioner who recommends rollover without the
documented analysis is exposed to ERISA §502(a) liability + DOL
EBSA enforcement + state-securities-fraud claim under the
narrow exception to ERISA §1144 preemption. The DOL Fiduciary
Rule litigation arc is load-bearing context: 2010 proposed →
2016 Obama-DOL final (the "Fiduciary Rule" expansion of fiduciary
duty to cover rollover-recommendations) → 2018 5th-Circuit
vacatur in *Chamber of Commerce v US Dept of Labor* (vacated the
2016 rule on Administrative Procedure Act grounds) → 2020 SEC
Reg-BI implementation (a *suitability-plus* standard for broker-
dealers, weaker than fiduciary) → 2024 DOL Retirement Security
Rule + PTE 2020-02 final (the second attempt at fiduciary-rule
expansion, more narrowly scoped to address the 5th-Circuit
critique) → ongoing 5th-Circuit litigation (the 2024 rule is
also being challenged in the 5th Circuit). The Form ADV Part 2
Brochure + Part 3 Form CRS reading procedure is the *consumer-
verification protocol*: before retaining any investment adviser,
verify on SEC IAPD adviserinfo.sec.gov (Form ADV Part 1 for
registration status + Part 2A Brochure for fees-and-compensation
+ Part 2B Brochure Supplement for advisor-credentials + Part 3
Form CRS for fee-and-conflict summary), and on FINRA
BrokerCheck for disciplinary-disclosure record (FINRA-registered
broker-dealers and registered representatives). The fee-only
firm declares no commission income in Form ADV Part 2A Item 5
*Fees and Compensation*; the fee-based firm declares both fee
and commission revenue lines — the structural-conflict-of-
interest distinction is in the Form ADV text, not in the
marketing material. The asker who retains an advisor without
the Form ADV + Form CRS + BrokerCheck verification protocol is
acting on the firm's marketing-material claim that may or may
not survive the regulatory-disclosure cross-check.

## Known blind spots OF this community

- **The ERISA-attorney community's practice depth is on
  ERISA-qualified-plan-side issues, not on the IRA-side
  post-rollover or the brokerage / IRA-after-distribution
  layer where the asker's funds live for the bulk of their
  retirement lifecycle.** ERISA §404 fiduciary duty attaches
  to the plan-sponsor / plan-administrator / plan-investment-
  manager + to investment-advisers providing fiduciary advice
  to the plan; once the asker's funds roll out of the
  ERISA-qualified plan into an IRA, ERISA stops applying and
  the IRA is governed by the Investment Advisers Act of 1940
  (SEC) + state-securities-law (in some cases). The ERISA-
  attorney community's practice is concentrated on the
  plan-side; the IRA-side scrutiny (advisor recommendations
  on the IRA balance, variable-annuity products inside the
  IRA, the AUM-fee compensation on the IRA balance) requires
  a *different* practitioner-class — typically a state-bar
  securities-arbitration attorney, the SEC Office of
  Investor Education and Advocacy, or the FINRA arbitration
  channel. Trigger: an asker with a fiduciary-breach concern
  on the IRA-side post-rollover consults an ERISA attorney
  whose practice is plan-side; the attorney correctly
  identifies that ERISA no longer applies but does not
  refer to the securities-arbitration channel. Failure mode:
  asker concludes there is no remedy and does not pursue
  the SEC / FINRA / state-securities-arbitration channel
  that does apply, the statute-of-limitations runs (typically
  2-5 years depending on the specific claim), the remedy
  window closes. Recovery: cross-reference against
  `personal-finance/sources.yaml` cross-community routing for
  the IRA-side arbitration channel — SEC Office of Investor
  Education and Advocacy (investor.gov complaint procedure);
  FINRA Office of Dispute Resolution Services (arbitration
  + mediation procedure); state-securities-administrator
  via NASAA (North American Securities Administrators
  Association) for state-securities-law claims; and route to
  a state-bar securities-arbitration attorney with PIABA
  (Public Investors Advocate Bar Association) membership —
  PIABA is the canonical plaintiff-side investor-protection
  bar association — per framings.md F10 selective-referral
  matrix and F14 advisor-fraud framing.

- **The DOL Fiduciary Rule litigation arc creates regulatory-
  uncertainty that the community routinely under-engages in
  household-decision framing.** The 2024 DOL Retirement
  Security Rule + PTE 2020-02 is being challenged in the
  5th Circuit (as the 2016 rule was), and the litigation
  outcome is uncertain — the practitioner community routinely
  describes the *current* rule-state without naming the
  *likely-vacatur-risk* in the asker's household-decision
  horizon. The asker who counts on the 2024 rule's
  documented-suitability-analysis requirement as a
  protection at retirement is exposed if the rule is
  vacated post-2024 and the practitioner reverts to the
  pre-rule advisory standard. Additionally, the SEC Reg-BI's
  *suitability-plus* standard is weaker than fiduciary and
  has been criticized as insufficient by consumer-protection
  advocates (Olen, CBPP, CFP Board's ongoing comment letters);
  the practitioner community's routine deployment of "Reg-BI
  applies" framing obscures the suitability-plus weakness.
  Trigger: a Cohen & Buckmann or Faegre Drinker blog post
  on the 2024 Retirement Security Rule cites the
  documented-suitability-analysis requirement as binding,
  without naming the 5th-Circuit-vacatur-risk in the
  out-years. Failure mode: asker relies on the rule's
  protection in retirement-planning, the rule is vacated
  in 2027, the advisor reverts to pre-rule standard, the
  asker's rollover-recommendation suitability is no longer
  documented and the asker has no claim. Recovery: cross-
  reference against `personal-finance/sources.yaml`
  community `financial-economics-journalism` (Tergesen at
  WSJ on DOL Retirement Security Rule litigation timeline;
  *InvestmentNews* fiduciary-rule beat for the trade-press
  coverage of the 5th-Circuit litigation) and require
  household-decision-planning to be robust to *either*
  rule-state — F14 policy-risk + advisor-fraud framing in
  framings.md.

- **The Form ADV Part 2 Brochure + Part 3 Form CRS reading
  procedure protects against the *registered* universe of
  investment advisers but does not catch unregistered
  scammers and *registered-with-no-history* new entities.**
  The SEC IAPD adviserinfo.sec.gov database includes
  SEC-registered investment-advisers (over $100M AUM) and
  state-registered investment-advisers (under $100M, state-
  by-state); FINRA BrokerCheck includes FINRA-registered
  broker-dealers and registered representatives. Both
  databases are *registration-based*: unregistered advisors
  (running illegally, sometimes operating as "consultants"
  to evade registration) are not searchable, and newly-
  registered advisors have no disclosure-history to surface
  before the first misconduct is reported. The Madoff fraud
  pattern (registered investment-adviser with no
  disclosure-history of misconduct until the fraud
  collapsed) is the canonical reminder that the
  registration-and-disclosure verification protocol catches
  *prior-history* but not *current-fraud-pre-detection*.
  Trigger: an asker verifies a new advisor on SEC IAPD,
  finds clean registration with no disciplinary disclosure,
  retains the advisor; the advisor is operating a Madoff-
  pattern fraud not yet detected. Failure mode: asker's
  assets are mis-allocated for years until the fraud is
  detected via external audit or SEC investigation; the
  recovery percentage in such cases is typically <30%.
  Recovery: cross-reference against `personal-finance/
  sources.yaml` community `financial-economics-journalism`
  (Zweig's *Intelligent Investor* archive on advisor-
  fraud patterns + ProPublica IRS / wealth-inequality
  investigative archive) and apply due-diligence beyond
  registration verification — third-party custodian
  verification (Schwab / Fidelity / Vanguard custody
  separate from advisor), audit-firm verification (PCAOB-
  registered audit firm independent of advisor), and the
  *separately managed account* structure that keeps the
  asker's assets at a third-party custodian rather than
  in a pooled-fund vehicle controlled by the advisor —
  F14 policy-risk + advisor-fraud framing in framings.md.

- **The ERISA-attorney community does not engage the
  behavioral / cashflow-stress reality of household
  retirement-planning and routinely defers to "consult a
  financial advisor" framing that the asker does not have
  the resources to act on.** ERISA litigation is high-cost
  to pursue (legal fees typically $25k-$100k+ for plan-
  participant litigation, with contingency-fee
  arrangements available but typically requiring high-
  damages exposure to justify); the typical household-
  retirement-planning asker faces fiduciary-breach
  concerns at scale orders-of-magnitude below the
  threshold that justifies retained-counsel pursuit. The
  ERISA-attorney community's blog-voice routinely
  describes the litigation-pathway without naming the
  cost-barrier that gates the pathway. Trigger: a
  Cohen & Buckmann blog post on a plan-administrator
  fiduciary-breach scenario describes the §502(a) civil
  enforcement procedure without naming the legal-fee
  cost. Failure mode: asker reads the blog, recognizes
  the situation in their own plan, attempts to retain
  counsel, finds the legal-fee cost prohibitive, takes
  no action, the statute-of-limitations runs. Recovery:
  cross-reference against the DOL EBSA's *askebsa.dol.
  gov* complaint procedure (administrative-channel,
  no legal-fees required), the *AARP Foundation
  Litigation* (legal-aid channel for retiree-fiduciary-
  breach issues), and *PIABA* (Public Investors Advocate
  Bar Association) for contingency-fee counsel referral
  on securities-arbitration claims — F10 selective-
  referral matrix in framings.md.

- **The ERISA-attorney community engages the *legal-
  framework* of fiduciary duty but routinely under-
  engages the *empirical / behavioral* reality of how
  fiduciary-violations actually materialize in household
  outcomes.** The legal framework (ERISA §404 +
  prohibited-transaction + §502(a) enforcement) is
  *structurally-sound* but the *empirical* reality is
  that most household-fiduciary-violations are not
  litigated — the asker doesn't detect the violation,
  doesn't pursue counsel, doesn't recover damages, and
  the practitioner who committed the violation continues
  to practice. The ERISA-attorney community's blog-voice
  routinely engages the legal-framework without naming
  the empirical-detection-gap and routinely defers to
  consumer-vigilance as the protection-channel without
  naming that consumer-vigilance is the *primary failure
  mode* (the asker doesn't have the technical literacy
  to detect the rollover-recommendation suitability
  failure or the variable-annuity fee-stack misalignment).
  Trigger: a Faegre Drinker *Best Interest* blog post on
  PTE 2020-02 compliance describes the documented-
  suitability-analysis requirement without naming the
  asker-detection-gap. Failure mode: asker doesn't
  detect the violation, doesn't trigger the enforcement
  channel, the violation continues at scale across the
  practitioner's book of business. Recovery: cross-
  reference against `personal-finance/sources.yaml`
  community `financial-economics-journalism` (Olen
  *Pound Foolish* + Zweig *Intelligent Investor* on the
  empirical-misconduct archive) and against the
  structural-reform proposals (CFP Board ongoing
  comment letters on Reg-BI's suitability-plus weakness;
  CBPP / TPC structural-policy critique) — empirical-
  detection requires the asker to engage the journalism-
  community archive plus the consumer-protection-
  organization channel, not just the ERISA-attorney-
  litigation channel — F14 advisor-fraud framing in
  framings.md.

## Mechanism E posture

Personal-finance is `high_stakes: true` per
`domain_knowledge/_meta_ontology.md` §5 and the canonical
Mechanism E gating case named in `docs/specs/ROADMAP.md` §5.
Every blindspot above ends with a Recovery move routed to a
named professional channel. For ERISA-and-fiduciary-rule-law-
sourced framings the default channel is a **retained ERISA-
side attorney with ERISA Title I + 401k-rollover-scrutiny +
QDRO-drafting + fiduciary-breach §404 / §405 practice depth**
— ABA Section on Labor and Employment Law / Employee Benefits
Committee member, ACEBC fellow, Wagner Law Group / Cohen &
Buckmann / Faegre Drinker / Drinker Biddle affiliation
preferred. Where the decision crosses into IRA-side post-
rollover (where ERISA no longer applies and the Investment
Advisers Act of 1940 + state-securities-law govern), Mechanism
E re-routes to a **state-bar securities-arbitration attorney
with PIABA (Public Investors Advocate Bar Association)
membership** for plaintiff-side investor-protection claims,
or to the **SEC Office of Investor Education and Advocacy**
(investor.gov complaint procedure) + **FINRA Office of
Dispute Resolution Services** for the arbitration / mediation
channel + the **state-securities-administrator via NASAA**
for state-securities-law claims. Where the decision crosses
into Form ADV verification (before retaining any investment
adviser), the procedure is: verify on **SEC IAPD
adviserinfo.sec.gov** for Form ADV Part 1 + Part 2A Brochure
+ Part 2B Brochure Supplement + Part 3 Form CRS, and on
**FINRA BrokerCheck brokercheck.finra.org** for disciplinary-
disclosure record; verify the firm declares no commission
income in Form ADV Part 2A Item 5 *Fees and Compensation* for
the fee-only structural-conflict-of-interest distinction; for
the legal-aid / contingency-fee channel where the household
cannot afford retained counsel, route to **DOL EBSA
askebsa.dol.gov** complaint procedure (administrative-channel
for ERISA-qualified-plan fiduciary-breach), the **AARP
Foundation Litigation** (legal-aid for retiree-fiduciary-
breach), and **PIABA** for contingency-fee counsel referral
on securities-arbitration claims. Where the decision crosses
into QDRO drafting on divorce, Mechanism E re-routes to a
**state-bar family-law attorney with QDRO-drafting practice
depth** (typically working in coordination with the ERISA
attorney for plan-document-specific QDRO requirements) — the
plan-document approves or rejects the QDRO based on very
specific statutory and regulatory requirements, and a QDRO
drafted by a generic family-law attorney without ERISA-
specific practice depth is routinely rejected by the plan-
administrator. Where the decision crosses into estate-
planning + beneficiary-designation audit, Mechanism E re-
routes to a **state-bar estate attorney** with the
beneficiary-designation update procedure coordinated with the
ERISA attorney for the plan-side and the IRA-custodian for
the IRA-side. For the asker's own fiduciary-rule-protection
verification, route to the **DOL EBSA *Compliance Assistance*
publications** (askebsa.dol.gov) and the **SEC Office of
Investor Education and Advocacy** (investor.gov) for the
public-procedure references. This community is the structural-
statutory-and-procedural framing layer; the retained named
professional with case-specific plan documents, Form 5500
filings, beneficiary forms, QDRO requirements, or fiduciary-
breach evidence on the table is the binding-determination layer.
