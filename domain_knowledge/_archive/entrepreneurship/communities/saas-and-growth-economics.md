## Mental model

- ARR is the load-bearing metric; NRR is the asymmetry
  multiplier. The community's mental model treats ARR (annual
  recurring revenue) as the single load-bearing metric — but
  *the same ARR* with NRR 110% vs NRR 90% produces radically
  different terminal enterprise value. The SaaStr / Tomasz
  Tunguz / a16z benchmark series anchor "best-in-class B2B
  SaaS NRR > 120% (expansion outpaces churn on existing
  cohort); good-not-great 100-120%; failing < 95% (cohort is
  net-shrinking and growth-from-new-logos must compensate)."
- CAC payback is the binding velocity constraint. The community
  reasons that CAC payback in months (CAC / gross-margin-
  adjusted ARR per customer per month) determines how fast a
  company can scale on a given amount of capital. SaaStr's
  canonical "12-month CAC payback is healthy SMB; 18-month
  is healthy mid-market; 24-month is the upper bound for
  enterprise where the LTV makes it work; > 36 months is
  unfundable except at very-high-NRR." Tomasz Tunguz's burn-
  multiple framing (burn / net-new ARR) is the data-driven
  complement: burn-multiple < 1.0 is best-in-class for a
  Series A-ready company, < 2.0 is acceptable, > 3.0 is a
  red flag.
- The $1-10M-ARR-is-the-hard-part framing is the SaaStr
  canonical mental model. The community's framing is that
  reaching $1M ARR is hard in a different way (PMF discovery,
  founder-led sales, hand-built distribution) than reaching
  $10M ARR (sales-team build-out, first-VPS / first-VPE / first-
  VPP hire, formal sales-and-marketing operations) than
  reaching $100M ARR (multi-product expansion, enterprise-
  sales motion, multi-segment GTM, international expansion).
  The phase-transition mechanics matter; the metric-targets
  shift across phases.
- PLG and SLG are different motions, not "better or worse"
  motions. Lenny Newsletter's framing is that product-led-
  growth (PLG — Slack / Notion / Figma / Loom archetype) and
  sales-led-growth (SLG — Salesforce / Workday / ServiceNow
  archetype) are distinct GTM motions with different unit-
  economics, hiring sequences, pricing-architecture, and
  category-fit. The Magic Number / Rule of 40 dogma is
  primarily SLG-calibrated; PLG-specific metrics (activation
  rate, free-to-paid conversion, viral coefficient, time-to-
  value) require a parallel discipline.
- Hiring sequence is the bottleneck at scale. First Round
  Review + SaaStr operator-narrative content anchor the
  hiring-as-bottleneck framing: founder-led-sales transition
  to first-AE around $500k-1M ARR with a "ramen-paid AE who
  closes for you" arrangement; first-CRO / VPS at $5-10M ARR
  to build the sales org; first-VPE at $3-8M ARR to scale
  engineering; first-CFO at $20-50M ARR. The timing and
  quality of each hire is a recurring blindspots.md trap
  (F10 / D7 cluster).

## Known blind spots OF this community

- **SaaS-metric dogma calibrated to SLG; PLG and consumption-
  pricing categories systematically mis-calibrated.** The
  community's canonical metric definitions (ARR / NRR / CAC
  payback / Magic Number / Rule of 40) are structurally
  sales-led-SaaS calibrated — they presuppose a contract-based
  revenue model with named accounts, a sales team that closes
  contracts, a renewal cycle with measurable expansion-vs-
  churn. PLG categories (self-serve sign-up, freemium-tier-
  conversion, viral acquisition) and consumption-pricing
  categories (Snowflake-archetype usage-based-revenue,
  OpenAI-API-token-pricing AI startups) have fundamentally
  different unit-economics topology — Tomasz Tunguz's AI-
  startup pricing-model analysis is the canonical practitioner
  series flagging this mis-calibration. Trigger: founder is
  building a PLG / freemium / consumption-priced product AND
  is benchmarking against SaaS-canonical CAC payback / Magic
  Number / NRR thresholds calibrated for SLG contract revenue.
  Failure mode: founder reports "our CAC payback is 6 months"
  on PLG self-serve and concludes the unit-economics are
  best-in-class, while the actual binding metric is freemium-
  to-paid conversion rate (typical PLG benchmark 2-5% on
  freemium, < 1% is sub-scale) and the CAC measurement is
  underestimating channel-acquisition cost (content + SEO +
  community + product-virality are blended into a low-CAC
  number that doesn't decompose cleanly); the company scales
  the conversion-rate weakness and discovers at $10M ARR that
  the activation-rate is 20% (vs benchmark 40-60%) and the
  retention curve is bending down. Recovery (self-directed):
  for PLG categories, use Lenny Newsletter PLG-specific
  benchmarks (activation rate, free-to-paid conversion, viral
  coefficient, time-to-value, retention curves at week-1 /
  month-1 / month-3 / month-6) as the load-bearing metrics
  AND treat ARR / Magic Number as secondary diagnostics; for
  consumption-priced categories, use Tomasz Tunguz's AI-
  startup pricing-model series and Bessemer consumption-
  pricing benchmarks; cross-reference with `indie-hackers-
  and-bootstrap` (Patio11 "pricing as load-bearing decision"
  + the value-based-pricing discipline) on the pricing-
  architecture choice that precedes the metric question.

- **$1-10M-ARR-is-the-hard-part framing under-engages with
  the $0-1M-ARR PMF-discovery cost.** The SaaStr canonical
  framing focuses on the post-PMF scaling phase ($1M-to-$10M-
  ARR scaling-mechanics) as the load-bearing problem and
  treats the pre-PMF phase as a precursor that the venture-
  track has already resolved. But the $0-to-$1M-ARR phase
  (PMF discovery, founder-led sales, hand-built distribution)
  is where the actual founder time-and-energy goes for
  18-24 months of the company's life, and the SaaStr voice
  systematically under-engages with the *what* of PMF
  discovery (the YC / Paul Graham / Patio11 voices dominate
  here). Trigger: founder is pre-PMF (no documented PMF
  signal — no 10-50 customers, no $20-100k MRR, no strong
  retention signal) AND is benchmarking the company against
  SaaStr canonical metrics. Failure mode: founder applies
  $1-10M scaling-discipline thinking to a $50k-MRR product
  that has not yet found PMF; hires a head-of-marketing and
  a first-AE; spends $400k on demand-gen against a product
  the market has not validated; 12 months later the company
  has $100k MRR with a $1M burn and the PMF discovery is
  no further along than at start. Recovery (self-directed):
  for pre-PMF founders, defer to `yc-and-vc-backed-playbooks`
  (Paul Graham *Do Things That Don't Scale* + Michael Seibel
  *How to Plan an MVP* + Dalton Caldwell *How to Talk to
  Users*) and `indie-hackers-and-bootstrap` (Patio11 + Rob
  Walling on customer-discovery and unit-economics-from-day-
  1); apply SaaStr scaling-mechanics only once PMF signal
  is documented (10-50 paying customers, NRR > 100% on the
  early cohort, organic growth signal independent of paid-
  acquisition).

- **Hiring-sequence framing structurally assumes venture-
  track capital availability and W-2-employee defaults; mis-
  applies in capital-constrained / contractor-default contexts.**
  The First Round Review + SaaStr operator-narrative on the
  hiring-sequence (BDR / SDR / AE / CSM at $500k-$1M ARR;
  first-VPS at $5-10M; first-VPE at $3-8M; first-CFO at $20-
  50M) presupposes (a) venture-track capital availability to
  fund the hires before the revenue catches up, (b) US-W-2-
  employee defaults with the associated payroll-tax / benefits
  / equity-compensation structure, (c) the asker is in a
  geography (US tech-hub) where the talent supply matches the
  framing. Founders in bootstrap-default contexts (Indie
  Hackers framing), in contractor-default contexts (1099 SDR
  / AE / CSM arrangements), or in non-US-tech-hub geographies
  receive a framing miscalibrated to their constraints —
  AND a recurring blindspots.md F7 / D7 trap on first-W-2-
  employee classification in CA / MA / NJ / IL ABC-test
  states where mis-applying "hire an SDR as a 1099" produces
  PAGA / Labor Code §203 penalties that compound on the
  underlying revenue-team-comp cost. Trigger: founder is
  reading SaaStr / First Round Review on first-revenue-hire
  timing AND is bootstrap-funded OR planning a 1099 BDR /
  SDR / AE arrangement OR is in a CA / MA / NJ / IL ABC-test
  state for the contractor classification. Failure mode:
  founder hires a 1099 BDR in CA on a commission-only basis;
  ABC-test enforcement classifies as misclassified W-2
  employee; the company faces backpay + payroll-tax-
  liability + PAGA penalties + Labor Code §203 waiting-time
  penalties on the original $80k commission paid out, scaling
  many-fold to the original commission. Recovery (self-
  directed framing; employment-law attorney for adjudication):
  for the hire-timing question, cross-reference with
  `indie-hackers-and-bootstrap` (Rob Walling's "consulting
  to product" + first-hire-as-cash-positive discipline) for
  bootstrap-default contexts; for the classification question,
  cross-reference with `legal-and-cap-table-mechanics` +
  retained employment-law attorney (especially in CA / MA /
  NJ / IL) BEFORE making any 1099 BDR / SDR / AE arrangement.

- **ARR-and-growth framing under-engages with profitability
  and the macro-cycle.** The SaaStr / Tomasz Tunguz framing
  evolved during the 2010-2021 zero-interest-rate environment
  where growth was rewarded with growth multiples and the
  profitability question was deferred to "later." The
  2022-2024 macro reversal (rate-hiking cycle + venture
  pullback + IPO window closure) revalued growth-at-any-cost
  and pulled the Rule of 40 / burn-multiple discipline from
  "best practice" to "Series-A entry requirement." Recent
  SaaStr / Tomasz Tunguz content has shifted heavily toward
  capital-efficiency framing, but the legacy "$1-10M-ARR-
  is-the-hard-part" content + the canonical hiring-sequence
  / growth-investment recommendations were authored in the
  2010-2021 regime and may not translate. Trigger: founder
  is reading SaaStr / First Round Review / Tomasz Tunguz
  content authored before 2022 AND is planning a fundraise
  / hiring / growth-investment cycle in a post-2022 macro
  regime. Failure mode: founder applies pre-2022 hiring-
  sequence and growth-investment timing in a post-2022
  market where capital-efficiency is the binding constraint;
  burn-multiple > 2.0 disqualifies the company from Series-A
  rounds; the company is forced into a flat-or-down round at
  punitive terms. Recovery (self-directed): for fundraise-
  and-hiring timing, weight recent (post-2022) SaaStr /
  Tomasz Tunguz / Bessemer content heavily and apply pre-
  2022 content only with explicit macro-regime translation;
  cross-reference with `yc-and-vc-backed-playbooks`
  (Founder Collective "venture as debt" + capital-efficiency-
  as-moat framing) and `indie-hackers-and-bootstrap`
  (capital-efficient bootstrap-track as the default for
  capital-constrained markets).

- **Anglo-American / SF-tech-network / English-language-
  content bias; geographic and category coverage uneven.**
  The SaaStr / Tomasz Tunguz / Lenny Newsletter / First
  Round Review content corpus is heavily over-indexed in
  US-headquartered B2B-SaaS companies with SF / NYC / Boston
  / Seattle operating bases; European / Asian / Latin-
  American SaaS context is under-represented; non-English-
  language content channels (German Saas-Hub, Japanese
  startup community, China cloud-software) are essentially
  absent from this community's reference set. Category bias
  toward dev-tools / AI / fintech / vertical-SaaS in the
  AI-and-cloud cycle; D2C / consumer-subscription / non-
  technical-buyer categories receive less precise framings.
  Trigger: founder is in a non-US geography OR a non-B2B-SaaS
  category (D2C subscription, consumer-prosumer, vertical-
  SaaS in healthcare / govtech / education / nonprofit where
  the buying motion has structural differences) AND is
  benchmarking against SaaStr / Tomasz Tunguz / Lenny content.
  Failure mode: the metric thresholds (CAC payback target,
  NRR benchmark, hiring-sequence timing) are mis-applied to
  a context with different unit-economics or different
  buying-motion mechanics; the founder mis-prioritizes the
  load-bearing problem. Recovery (self-directed): for non-
  US geographies, cross-reference with regional SaaS
  communities (SaaStr Europa / Asia content, Point Nine
  Capital benchmarks for Europe, Bessemer Cloud Index
  Anglosphere benchmarks); for non-B2B-SaaS categories,
  cross-reference with category-specific communities
  (Indie Hackers for consumer-subscription, r/sweatystartup
  for services-business — see `r-entrepreneur-and-small-
  business`); apply SaaStr-canonical metrics only where the
  category genuinely is B2B-SaaS-with-contract-revenue.

  LLC-vs-S-corp election at the $80-100k SE-tax crossover
  (rare in pure-venture-track contexts; common in pre-formation
  consulting / freelance context), **D8** retirement-vehicle
  selection (solo-401(k) / SEP-IRA / SIMPLE / 401(k) at the
  company level — the company-level 401(k) vs SEP-IRA choice
  is part of the canonical first-HR-systems checklist around
  $1-5M ARR), and any multi-state-nexus boundary crossing
  (especially for SaaS companies with remote distributed
  teams in multiple states).
- **Employment-law attorney** for **D7** first W-2 employee
  classification decisions — **highest priority on the BDR /
  SDR / AE / CSM commission-and-classification question in
  CA / MA / NJ / IL ABC-test states** where mis-classifying
  a contractor-default revenue-team-role can compound
  penalties many-fold on top of the standard revenue-team-
  comp arithmetic (backpay + payroll-tax-liability + PAGA
  exposure + Labor Code §203 waiting-time penalties + attorney
  fees + interest); and **D11** founder-departure with non-
  compete / non-solicit / IP-assignment disputes.

  lookup** as the $0-friction procedural floor on any
  individual professional recommended.
