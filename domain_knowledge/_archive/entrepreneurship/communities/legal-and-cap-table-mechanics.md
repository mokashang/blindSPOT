## Mental model

- Cap-table is the founder's load-bearing artifact. The
  community's mental model treats the cap-table as the
  document-of-record that captures every equity decision
  across the company's life — founder splits, advisor grants,
  option-pool reservations, SAFE issuances, priced-round
  conversions, secondary transactions, founder departures.
  The cap-table is the source-of-truth that every subsequent
  decision references; mistakes don't reverse cleanly
  (issued equity is effectively impossible to claw back
  without consideration or for-cause termination — over-
  granting advisor or co-founder equity in the first 90
  days is the modal founder regret per `framings.md` /
  `decisions.md`).
- The 4-year-vest-with-1-year-cliff is template; departures
  and additions are case-specific. The vesting structure is
  the founder-side protection against the modal co-founder-
  departure outcome (one founder leaves at month 18 with
  18-of-48 months vested — 37.5% — and 62.5% returns to the
  pool for future hires or remaining founders). Post-
  incorporation co-founder addition is case-specific: do
  they get founder-share-class with the same 4-year
  schedule starting on the join date? Do they get any
  vesting credit for pre-join contribution? Are they on
  a separate vesting schedule? These are recurring
  blindspots.md F3 cluster traps where the retained
  attorney adjudicates.
- The 83(b) election is a 30-day-from-grant window with no
  extension and no recovery mechanism. The community is
  sharp on this: file 83(b) within 30 days of stock grant,
  via certified mail with USPS Form 3811 return receipt
  (the canonical evidence-of-filing artifact), with a
  copy to the company's records and a copy attached to
  the founder's first 1040 covering the grant year. Missing
  the 83(b) window converts each future vesting tranche
  into ordinary-income at then-current FMV, which can
  approach 50%+ of equity value at a high-growth exit (a
  tax-poison-pill that has killed founder net-worth on
  otherwise-successful outcomes per `framings.md` F3 /
  `blindspots.md` F3 cluster).
- The QSBS Section-1202 5-year-hold-period starts at
  C-corp stock issuance. NOT at SAFE issuance, NOT at LLC
  formation, NOT at the founder's first day of work. The
  community is sharp on this: 18-24 months of SAFE-sitting
  silently delays the QSBS clock with no warning from
  generic CPAs (per `framings.md` F5 / `blindspots.md` F5
  cluster). The LLC-to-C-corp §351 conversion with QSBS
  clock-start is case-specific and requires startup-
  experienced CPA + attorney coordination. The redemption-
  test for QSBS qualification (redemptions in the 4-year
  window before issuance + 2-year window after disqualify
  shares from QSBS) is a recurring blindspots.md F5 trap.
- Post-money YC SAFE is template; SAFE-stacks are case-
  specific. The community is sharp on the YC SAFE template
  mechanics: post-money valuation cap, MFN clause behavior,
  conversion-on-priced-round mechanics. The trap is in
  stacking — 5+ SAFEs at various caps with MFN clauses
  auto-upgrade to the best subsequent terms; the cumulative
  conversion-on-Series-A dilution is routinely mis-modeled
  by founders (per `framings.md` F1 / `blindspots.md` F1.3
  cluster). The case-specific cap-table model is firmly
  attorney territory.

## Known blind spots OF this community

- **Published content is deliberately non-adjudicative; the
  firm's business model monetizes the retainer.** Cooley GO,
  Orrick OpenLP, WSGR / Gunderson selected publications, and
  Clerky-platform content are *orientation-and-pattern-
  recognition* sources — the firms' business model
  monetizes the case-specific adjudication via retainer,
  and the published content is calibrated to (a) attract
  prospective clients, (b) not constitute attorney-client
  advice on case-specific questions, (c) leave the
  high-margin adjudication work to the retainer
  relationship. Trigger: asker is treating a Cooley GO /
  Orrick OpenLP / Clerky-platform blog post as adjudicative
  guidance on a case-specific D1 / D5 / D11 / QSBS /
  IP-assignment question. Failure mode: asker proceeds on
  the orientation-content interpretation; the case-specific
  detail (post-incorporation co-founder addition with prior-
  IP-contribution; SAFE-stack with various caps and MFN
  clauses; QSBS clock-start on §351 conversion with
  pre-conversion service-based stock issuance; IP-assignment
  carve-out from a prior W-2 employer in a state with
  inventions-assignment statute) lands wrong; the cost
  surfaces at exit (QSBS disqualification on $10M+ gain =
  $2.5-4M federal tax + state tax; IP-assignment dispute
  with prior employer at acquisition = entire founder
  proceeds at stake; SAFE-stack mis-conversion = 5-15%
  extra dilution permanently). Recovery (Mechanism E —
  retained startup attorney): for any D1 / D5 / D11 /
  QSBS / IP-assignment / multi-SAFE / §351-conversion /
  non-standard-vesting question, the Cooley GO / Orrick
  OpenLP / Clerky platform is the *starting point* for
  the asker's own preparation BEFORE the attorney
  consultation, NOT the answer. Founder-side counsel is the
  canonical posture (separate from investor-side counsel);
  Clerky-platform engages partner attorneys for case-
  specific questions outside the platform's template
  mechanics; the high-end firms (Cooley / Gunderson / WSGR /
  Orrick / Fenwick) have founder-side and venture-side
  practices and the case-specific consultation should
  match founder-side. State Bar lookup on any individual
  attorney recommended is the $0-friction procedural floor.

- **Template-driven thinking under-engages with non-standard
  founder-formation patterns.** Clerky + Stripe Atlas are
  excellent at the *standard* YC-track Delaware C-corp
  formation — but the templates implicitly assume (a) two-
  to-four co-founders splitting on join, (b) clean IP-
  assignment from each founder's prior W-2 employer (or
  no prior W-2 with assignable IP), (c) US-resident
  founders or non-US-resident founders fitting the Stripe
  Atlas non-US-resident form-set, (d) a single-class
  founder-share structure. Non-standard patterns that the
  templates handle poorly: post-incorporation co-founder
  add with prior-IP-contribution (requires custom share-
  grant + acknowledgement + vesting credit modeling); IP-
  assignment carve-out from a prior W-2 employer with
  state-specific inventions-assignment statute (CA Labor
  Code §2870 carve-out for non-employer-time / non-
  employer-resources / non-employer-IP-relating work, but
  case-specific scope is litigated); dual-class share
  structure (founder-supervoting vs investor-non-
  supervoting — increasingly common in late-2010s and
  2020s); non-US-resident founders not fitting the
  Stripe Atlas form-set (some jurisdictions have specific
  treatment that Stripe Atlas's standard doesn't cover);
  international tax-treaty interaction (where Patio11's
  Kalzumeus cross-border tax-treaty content is the
  starting reference but case-specific is firmly attorney +
  tax-attorney territory). Trigger: asker is in a non-
  standard founder-formation pattern AND is relying on
  Clerky / Stripe Atlas template mechanics without case-
  specific attorney review. Failure mode: the formation
  proceeds on template-mechanics; the non-standard pattern
  lands wrong (the post-incorporation co-founder add
  produces a tax-treatment dispute at the IRS audit; the
  prior-W-2-employer IP claim at the acquisition stage is
  not adequately defended; the dual-class structure
  produces a Series-A protective-provision interaction the
  template-default does not handle; the non-US-resident
  formation triggers a CFC / PFIC / GILTI tax exposure
  the standard form-set does not warn about). Recovery
  (Mechanism E — retained startup attorney): for any non-
  standard founder-formation pattern (post-incorporation
  co-founder add; IP-assignment carve-out; dual-class
  shares; non-US-resident outside Stripe Atlas's form-set;
  international tax-treaty interaction), engage retained
  startup attorney BEFORE the formation rather than
  retrospectively; the Clerky / Stripe Atlas platform
  remains useful for the standard mechanics that DO
  template, but the non-standard layer requires attorney
  adjudication.

- **Venture-track-default in firm-side content; non-venture
  formation under-served.** The high-end firms (Cooley /
  Gunderson / WSGR / Orrick / Fenwick) overwhelmingly
  serve venture-track companies; their published content
  is venture-track-default; their case-specific consultation
  pricing is venture-track-calibrated (5-20k+ for formation
  with retained-counsel engagement). Non-venture formation
  contexts — bootstrap-track companies that don't plan to
  raise, services / consulting LLCs that don't form C-corps,
  small-business formations where the asker needs a $200-
  500 formation rather than a $5-20k formation — are
  systematically under-served by the high-end-firm content
  AND the accessible-end firms (Atomic / Mintz / Clerky-
  coordinated) have a similar venture-track-bias even at
  the accessible price point. Trigger: asker is in a
  bootstrap-track / services / non-venture-formation
  context AND is reading Cooley GO / Orrick OpenLP /
  WSGR / Gunderson content calibrated to venture-track
  formation. Failure mode: asker over-engineers the
  formation (Delaware C-corp + YC SAFE-template-ready when
  the actual business is a single-state services LLC),
  pays $5-15k for unnecessary venture-track formation
  mechanics, and faces ongoing administrative cost
  (Delaware franchise tax, annual filing, registered-
  agent fees) that the actual business model doesn't
  justify. Recovery (self-directed for the bootstrap /
  services formation choice; selective Mechanism E for
  case-specific edge cases): cross-reference with
  `indie-hackers-and-bootstrap` (Patio11 / Rob Walling /
  TinySeed content on bootstrap-track formation
  considerations + Clerky / Stripe Atlas standard
  formation at the accessible price point) and `r-
  entrepreneur-and-small-business` (r/smallbusiness /
  r/Accounting threads on services-LLC formation + state-
  specific entity considerations) for non-venture
  contexts; engage retained attorney only for the case-
  specific adjudication points that genuinely require it,
  not for the formation choice itself when the bootstrap-
  template suffices.

- **Cap-table modeling treated as deterministic when later-
  round behavior is path-dependent.** The community's
  cap-table mental model is structurally deterministic —
  the SAFE-to-priced-seed conversion math, the pre-money-
  to-post-money dilution arithmetic, the option-pool-
  refresh-on-Series-A mechanics are presented as derivable
  from the term sheet. But the realized cap-table at
  Series B and beyond is highly path-dependent on (a) the
  investor mix in earlier rounds (the lead investor's
  protective-provision style propagates), (b) the
  cumulative pro-rata-side-letter behavior from earlier
  investors (compounds the dilution math), (c) the option-
  pool-refresh negotiation at each round (commonly
  refreshed BEFORE the round close, which shifts the
  pre-money dilution to existing holders rather than the
  new investor), (d) the secondary-transaction history
  (founder secondary at Series B materially shifts the
  cap-table in ways the template models don't show by
  default). Trigger: asker is modeling Series B dilution
  from a Series A term sheet AND is using deterministic
  cap-table math without modeling the path-dependent
  interactions (pro-rata stack, option-pool-refresh
  pre-vs-post-money allocation, secondary-transaction
  capacity). Failure mode: founder reaches Series B with
  10-20% MORE cumulative dilution than the deterministic
  model predicted; surprised by the protective-provision
  lock-in from earlier rounds (per `blindspots.md` F1.3
  cluster); discovers at acquisition that the secondary-
  transaction capacity is bound by earlier-round consent
  rights the founder didn't notice in the term sheet.
  Recovery (Mechanism E — retained startup attorney):
  for any Series A / B priced round, require a path-
  dependent cap-table-and-protective-provision model from
  retained founder-side counsel that includes (1) pro-
  rata-stack behavior from prior investors, (2) option-
  pool-refresh negotiation framing pre-vs-post-money,
  (3) cumulative protective-provision interaction across
  rounds, (4) secondary-transaction capacity given
  prior-round consent rights. The Cooley GO / Orrick
  OpenLP content is the orientation; the case-specific
  modeling is attorney territory; Founder Collective
  voice on protective-provision compounding (`yc-and-vc-
  backed-playbooks` cross-reference) is the structural
  reminder of why this matters.

- **§83(b) election + QSBS clock-start mechanics rely on
  founder-procedural-discipline that systematically
  fails.** The 83(b) election is a 30-day-from-grant
  window with no extension and no recovery — but the
  procedural mechanics (file via certified mail with USPS
  Form 3811 return receipt; copy to company records; copy
  attached to first 1040 covering the grant year) require
  founder-procedural-discipline that systematically fails:
  founders file via regular USPS mail (no proof of timely
  filing on audit); founders file electronically (some
  IRS forms now permit electronic filing but 83(b)
  historically requires paper certified mail and the
  electronic-filing posture is recently clarified and
  procedural variance remains); founders fail to attach
  to the first 1040; founders lose the certified-mail
  receipt; founders forget the election on a stock-grant
  that was an "informal handshake" before formal
  documentation. The QSBS clock-start has similar
  procedural-discipline failures: founders treat SAFE
  issuance as starting the QSBS clock (it doesn't —
  clock starts at C-corp stock issuance); founders treat
  LLC formation as starting the clock (it doesn't —
  clock starts on conversion to C-corp); founders skip
  the §351 conversion paperwork and lose the QSBS
  qualification on conversion. Trigger: asker has a
  founder-grant or option-exercise event where 83(b) is
  required OR has SAFE / LLC / §351 conversion path where
  QSBS clock matters AND is relying on founder-procedural-
  discipline alone. Failure mode: 83(b) miss converts
  vesting tranches to ordinary-income-at-FMV (50%+ of
  equity value at a high-growth exit); QSBS clock miss
  costs $2.5-4M federal tax on $10M gain that would have
  qualified for exclusion; the procedural-failure cost
  surfaces at exit when the founder discovers the
  mechanics weren't followed. Recovery (Mechanism E —
  retained startup attorney + tax attorney coordination
  for QSBS; Clerky platform for the standard 83(b)
  procedural mechanics): for ANY founder-grant or option-
  exercise event, use Clerky's certified-mail 83(b)
  procedural template AND verify the certified-mail
  receipt and 1040 attachment occurred (or have retained
  attorney verify); for any SAFE → C-corp / LLC → C-corp /
  §351 conversion path, retain attorney + tax-attorney
  coordination BEFORE the conversion event to document
  the QSBS clock-start procedurally; cross-reference
  with `tax-and-entity-structure-cpa` for the IRS-Pub
  authority on the underlying tax mechanics.

## Mechanism E posture

**Mechanism E inline-routing on every D1 / D5 / D11 / QSBS /
IP-assignment decision** per
`domain_knowledge/entrepreneurship/blindspots.md`. This is the
stronger Recovery-routing posture (cf. `tax-and-entity-
structure-cpa`) for the entrepreneurship-domain because the
six-figure-tail-risk concentration in legal / cap-table
decisions is high enough that the published Cooley GO /
Orrick OpenLP / Clerky-platform content cannot substitute
for retained-attorney adjudication on case-specific facts.

- **Retained startup attorney with founder-side experience
  REQUIRED** for:
  - **D1** co-founder equity + vesting + IP-assignment
    structuring — case-specific facts on post-incorporation
    addition, prior-IP-contribution, state-specific
    inventions-assignment-statute interpretation.
  - **D5** SAFE post-money cap vs priced seed mechanics —
    case-specific facts on SAFE-stack-with-various-caps,
    MFN clause interaction, cumulative-conversion modeling,
    pro-rata-side-letter behavior across rounds.
  - **D11** founder-departure with > 10% equity at stake —
    cap-table buyback mechanics, separation-with-attorney-
    on-BOTH-sides default per `blindspots.md`,
    non-compete / non-solicit / IP-trailing claim
    adjudication.
  - **QSBS Section-1202** procedural floor on case-specific
    facts — C-corp issuance date verification, §351
    conversion mechanics on LLC-to-C-corp, redemption-
    test evaluation, federal-state divergence (CA does
    NOT conform; NY and NJ do not fully conform).
  - **IP-assignment carve-out from prior W-2 employer**
    — CA Labor Code §2870 inventions carve-out for non-
    employer-time / non-employer-resources / non-
    employer-IP-relating work, but case-specific scope is
    litigated; cross-domain edge to `tech-career`.
  - **Firm sourcing**: Cooley / Gunderson / WSGR / Orrick /
    Fenwick at the high-end (5-20k+ formation + ongoing
    retainer); Clerky-coordinated attorneys + Atomic +
    Mintz at the accessible-end (1-5k formation + on-demand
    retainer). Founder-side counsel separate from any
    investor-recommended counsel is the canonical posture
    on D5 priced-round / D11 departure.
- **Employment-law attorney** for **D7** first W-2 employee
  classification (especially in CA / MA / NJ / IL ABC-test
  states), and **D11** founder-departure with non-compete /
  non-solicit disputes. Cooley GO + Orrick OpenLP have
  published content on FTC 2024 non-compete rule status (in
  litigation per `blindspots.md` F7.3) — orientation only;
  case-specific adjudication requires retained employment-
  law attorney.
- **Tax attorney (separate from CPA) with §1202 / §351
  experience** for any QSBS clock-start adjudication, LLC-
  to-C-corp §351 conversion, or non-standard tax-mechanic
  question that exceeds the S-corp-CPA scope (cross-
  reference `tax-and-entity-structure-cpa` for the IRS-Pub
  authority + S-corp-CPA channel; tax attorney is the
  appropriate channel for the §1202 / §351 case-specific
  adjudication that S-corp-CPAs do not routinely handle).
- **State Bar / FINRA BrokerCheck / state-bar-grievance
  lookup** as the $0-friction procedural floor on any
  individual attorney recommended — verify license and
  disciplinary history before engagement; cross-references
  `personal-finance/communities/erisa-and-fiduciary-rule-
  law` + `decisions.md` selective referral category on
  this same procedural floor.

The published Cooley GO / Orrick OpenLP / WSGR / Gunderson /
Clerky-platform content remains useful as **orientation +
pattern-recognition + asker preparation BEFORE attorney
consultation**, but NOT as substitute for the retained-
attorney case-specific adjudication. For the standard YC-
track Delaware C-corp formation with no non-standard pattern,
Clerky-platform mechanics can carry the formation; for any
deviation from standard, retained attorney is the procedural
floor.
