The IRS-publication-authority + The Finance Buff procedural floor +
Nolo entity-structure voice — IRS Publications canonical
entrepreneurship / self-employed set (Pub 535 business expenses,
Pub 583 starting a business, Pub 587 home-office deduction, Pub
463 travel / entertainment, Pub 15 employer-tax-guide, Pub 560
retirement plans for small business), Schedule C / Schedule SE
official instructions, Harry Sit's *The Finance Buff*
(thefinancebuff.com — solo-401(k) plan-document analysis, SEP-

distribution arithmetic), Nolo small-business + tax guides
(nolo.com — lawyer-edited entity-structure references that bridge
IRS-Pub authority to operator workflow), Watson v US 2012
reasonable-salary precedent + IRS audit-guidance interpretive

floor mechanics on D4 (LLC pass-through vs S-corp election + multi-
state nexus + SSTB phaseout), D8 (solo-401(k) vs SEP-IRA vs SIMPLE
retirement vehicle coupled with S-corp election arithmetic), D7

Circular E + ABC-test state enforcement variance), home-office
substantiation (Pub 587), travel substantiation (Pub 463), and the
liability-shield framing's IRC §6672 trust-fund-tax-rules statutory
baseline. **It is fundamentally a referral source on case-specific
adjudication, not an adjudicator itself.** Per

case-specific facts.

## Mental model

- IRS Pub authority is procedural floor, not adjudication.
  The community's mental model treats IRS Pubs + Schedule
  C / SE instructions + Rev. Procs. as the *authoritative
  statutory ground-truth* that establishes what the rules
  are — but case-specific adjudication (what the rules
  *apply to* given the asker's particular facts) is firmly
  CPA / tax-attorney territory. The Pub authority anchors
  the conversation; the retained CPA models the application
  to the asker's facts.
- The $80-100k SE-tax crossover is the canonical S-corp
  election threshold, AT specific revenue / state /
  payroll / SSTB-status combinations. The community is
  sharp on this: below ~$80k net SE income, S-corp
  administrative cost (payroll setup + Form 1120-S + Form
  941 quarterly + reasonable-salary defensibility +
  potential state-franchise-tax — CA $800 LLC tax / NY
  filing-fee LLC publication / TX franchise tax) outweighs
  the SE-tax savings on the distribution portion; above
  ~$80-100k AND in a state without S-corp-specific friction
  (CA / NY / NJ / IL have varying frictions) AND in an
  SSTB-qualifying QBI context, the S-corp election typically
  pencils — but the case-specific adjudication on
  reasonable salary (Watson v US 2012 industry-comp data),
  the multi-state nexus interaction, the retirement-
  contribution-capacity trade-off (S-corp election destroys
  SEP-IRA contribution capacity by 30-40% per The Finance
  Buff F4.2 trap), and the QBI Section-199A SSTB-phaseout
  interaction (F4.1 trap) all require retained S-corp-
  experienced CPA.
- Watson v US 2012 sets the reasonable-salary defensibility
  floor. The community is sharp: S-corp owners cannot pay
  themselves $0 W-2 + 100% distribution to escape FICA
  (Watson sets the audit-recharacterization precedent —
  IRS recharacterized $24k W-2 + $300k distribution to
  $93k W-2 + reduced distribution with backpay + penalties +
  interest). The reasonable-salary is industry-comp + role-
  comp + market-comp data-driven; RCReports automated
  report is starting point; the case-specific defensibility
  requires retained S-corp-experienced CPA who can produce
  the supporting documentation that survives audit.
- Solo-401(k) plan-document mechanics determine Mega-
  Backdoor-Roth eligibility. The community is sharp: Harry
  Sit's *The Finance Buff* anchors the procedural floor —
  the canned solo-401(k) providers (Fidelity / Schwab /
  Vanguard) do NOT permit after-tax (non-Roth) contributions
  or in-service rollovers needed for Mega-Backdoor-Roth on
  the solo-401(k); custom plan-document from MySolo401k
  (mysolo401k.net) or Ascensus (or comparable third-party
  administrator) is required. The SEP-IRA-vs-solo-401(k)
  pro-rata-trap on backdoor-Roth is the recurring blindspots.md
  D8 cluster trap (existing SEP-IRA balance from earlier
  freelance year pollutes the backdoor-Roth pro-rata
  calculation under Notice 87-13 / IRC §408(d)(2)).
- IRC §6672 trust-fund-tax-rules pierce the LLC veil for
  unpaid payroll taxes. The community is sharp: the LLC /
  S-corp liability-shield does NOT protect against
  responsible-person penalties for unpaid trust-fund taxes
  (FICA + federal income tax withheld). The IRS can pierce
  the entity veil under §6672 to collect from the
  "responsible person" — which can be the founder, the
  CFO, or anyone with check-signing authority. Per
  `framings.md` F6 / `blindspots.md` F6.3 cluster: the
  liability-shield framing's statutory baseline is
  Pub 15 / Pub 583 covering basic employment-tax
  obligations; the §6672 trust-fund-tax-rules are the
  recurring trap.

## Known blind spots OF this community

- **IRS Pub authority is statutory ground-truth but does not
  resolve case-specific adjudication.** The IRS Pubs +
  Schedule C / SE instructions are the procedural floor
  on what the rules are — but the rules' application to
  the asker's facts is case-specific. Trigger: asker is
  treating IRS Pub citation as adjudicative on a question
  that depends on case-specific facts (the reasonable-
  salary range on the asker's specific role + industry +
  geography; the SSTB phaseout treatment on the asker's
  specific business activity; the multi-state nexus
  treatment on the asker's specific employee-location +
  customer-location mix; the home-office regular-and-
  exclusive-use defensibility on the asker's specific
  space and use pattern). Failure mode: asker proceeds on
  Pub-citation interpretation; case-specific adjudication
  point lands wrong (Watson reasonable-salary
  recharacterization at audit with back-FICA + penalties +
  interest; SSTB-phaseout mis-application costing $5-15k
  in lost QBI deduction; multi-state-nexus mis-application
  producing state-tax-agency assessment with penalties).
  Recovery (selective Mechanism E — retained S-corp-
  experienced CPA per `decisions.md`): for any D4 / D7 /
  D8 case-specific adjudication, the IRS Pub citation is
  the *starting point* for the asker's own preparation
  BEFORE the CPA consultation, NOT the answer; the retained
  CPA produces the audit-defensible documentation that the
  Pub citation alone cannot.

- **The Finance Buff voice is procedural-floor on the
  *mechanics* but not on the case-specific *application*.**
  Harry Sit's *The Finance Buff* content is the canonical
  practitioner outlet for solo-401(k) / SEP-IRA / Mega-
  Backdoor-Roth mechanics; the procedural detail is
  authoritative on the *what* (e.g. "the canned solo-401(k)
  providers do NOT permit after-tax contributions; custom
  plan-document from MySolo401k or Ascensus is required").
  But the case-specific application — does the asker's
  specific SEP-IRA balance pollute the backdoor-Roth via
  Notice 87-13 pro-rata? what's the *audit-defensible*
  reasonable-salary for the asker's specific role and
  industry? does the asker's specific multi-state-employee
  arrangement trigger state-tax-agency nexus? — is firmly
  retained-S-corp-experienced-CPA territory. Trigger: asker
  is treating *The Finance Buff* blog post as adjudicative
  on a case-specific application that requires fact-
  specific judgment. Failure mode: asker proceeds on
  *Finance Buff* mechanics without the case-specific
  adjudication; the pro-rata-trap fires at year-end Form
  8606 reconciliation; the reasonable-salary defense
  fails at audit; the multi-state-nexus situation has not
  been resolved before the state-tax-agency assessment.
  Recovery (selective Mechanism E — retained S-corp-
  experienced CPA): use *The Finance Buff* + Pub 560 as
  the procedural floor for the asker's own preparation;
  retain CPA for case-specific adjudication with the
  asker's actual Form 8606 history, current SEP / IRA
  balances, plan-document choice, S-corp election history,
  industry-comp data, and multi-state-employee
  configuration.

- **State-tax-agency divergence from federal IRS treatment
  is sharp; federal-only framing under-engages.** Several
  states diverge significantly from the federal IRS
  treatment of S-corp election, QBI, and multi-state
  nexus: CA recognizes S-corp election but imposes 1.5%
  S-corp franchise tax (minimum $800) AND has the $800
  LLC franchise tax that the federal-only analysis
  ignores; NY recognizes S-corp election but requires
  separate Form CT-6 election with mid-year cutoff
  variance; NJ has S-corp-specific filing-fee variance;
  TX has the franchise tax (margin tax) that applies to
  LLCs / S-corps / C-corps with no income-tax-based
  exception; FL has no income tax but has corporate
  filing fees; WA has B&O tax that operates parallel
  to federal income tax; the "convenience of employer"
  rule in NY / NJ / NE / DE / PA produces double-state-
  withholding situations for remote-worker arrangements.
  QBI Section-199A federal-state divergence is similarly
  sharp (CA does NOT conform to QBI; several other states
  partially conform). Trigger: asker is in a state with
  significant federal-state divergence AND is relying on
  federal-IRS-Pub framing without state-tax-agency
  consideration. Failure mode: asker proceeds on federal-
  only analysis; state-tax-agency assessment compounds on
  the federal treatment producing 1.5-3x the expected
  total state-and-federal tax burden; the multi-state
  remote-employee situation produces double-state-
  withholding the asker has not modeled; CA-domiciled
  S-corp owner discovers the 1.5% S-corp franchise tax
  + $800 LLC tax compresses the federal SE-tax savings
  to where the crossover is materially higher than the
  $80-100k federal threshold. Recovery (Mechanism E —
  retained S-corp-experienced / multi-state-nexus CPA):
  for any S-corp election decision in CA / NY / NJ / IL,
  for any multi-state remote-employee arrangement, for
  any QBI-significant business activity in a non-
  conforming state, retain CPA with current state-tax-
  agency-specific facts BEFORE the election or
  arrangement; cross-reference with state-tax-agency
  direct guidance (CA FTB, NY DTF, TX Comptroller) AND
  AICPA / state-CPA-society practitioner literature on
  the specific state divergence.

- **Reasonable-salary adjudication is industry-comp + role-
  comp + market-comp data-driven; RCReports automated
  output is starting point, not defensible answer alone.**
  The Watson v US 2012 + IRS audit-guidance precedent
  establishes that S-corp owners cannot pay themselves
  $0 W-2 + 100% distribution; the reasonable-salary must
  reflect the *role's compensation in arm's-length labor
  market*. RCReports (rcreports.com) is a popular S-corp-
  reasonable-salary report-generation tool with automated
  output that founders sometimes treat as the audit-
  defensible answer — but the automated output alone is
  insufficient: Watson-defensibility requires (a) industry-
  specific compensation data (RCReports pulls from BLS +
  PayScale + comparable sources), (b) role-specific
  description matching the founder's actual W-2 work
  vs distribution-portion-earned work (sometimes the
  founder's role spans CEO + Sales + Engineering, each
  with different comp data and the W-2 allocation must
  reflect the work-actually-done not the role-title alone),
  (c) market-comp adjustment for geography (RCReports
  defaults can over- or under-state for the asker's
  specific metro), (d) qualitative justification beyond
  the report. Per `framings.md` F4.3 + `blindspots.md`
  F4 cluster, the RCReports-is-insufficient-alone framing
  is the recurring trap. Trigger: asker is relying on
  RCReports automated output alone for the W-2-vs-
  distribution allocation on a high-income S-corp election
  AND has not engaged retained S-corp-experienced CPA for
  case-specific reasonable-salary documentation. Failure
  mode: at IRS audit, the RCReports output is presented;
  the auditor recharacterizes the W-2 portion upward;
  back-FICA + penalties + interest on the recharacterized
  amount; the audit-defense cost stacks on the underlying
  recharacterization. Recovery (selective Mechanism E —
  retained S-corp-experienced CPA): use RCReports as
  starting point for the asker's own preparation; retain
  CPA who produces the case-specific reasonable-salary
  documentation including industry-comp data, role-comp
  data, market-comp adjustment, and qualitative
  justification that survives audit.

- **QBI Section-199A SSTB-phaseout interaction with W-2
  salary is structurally complex and routinely mis-modeled.**
  The QBI Section-199A deduction (up to 20% of qualified
  business income) is reduced for SSTB ("specified service
  trade or business" — including consulting, financial
  services, health, law, accounting, performing arts,
  athletics, and any trade where the principal asset is
  the reputation or skill of one or more employees) when
  taxable income exceeds the phaseout threshold ($191,950
  single / $383,900 MFJ for 2024, with full phaseout at
  $241,950 / $483,900). The interaction with S-corp
  election + W-2 salary is structurally complex: (a)
  electing S-corp creates a W-2 wage component that
  potentially increases the W-2 wage limitation for non-
  SSTB QBI but does NOT help SSTB businesses past the
  phaseout (SSTB is fully disqualified at full phaseout
  regardless of W-2 wages); (b) the W-2 portion is
  subject to FICA + Medicare but is not QBI-eligible
  (only the distribution portion is QBI); (c) the
  interaction with multi-state taxation produces
  divergent state-level QBI treatment. Per `framings.md`
  F4 / `blindspots.md` F4.1 cluster, the SSTB-phaseout
  interaction is the recurring trap. Trigger: asker is
  in an SSTB context (consulting / professional services
  / financial services / health / law / accounting) AND
  is approaching or in the QBI phaseout band AND is
  modeling S-corp election impact on QBI without case-
  specific adjudication. Failure mode: asker elects
  S-corp expecting QBI benefit on the distribution
  portion; SSTB phaseout disqualifies the QBI entirely
  at full phaseout; the SE-tax savings on the
  distribution portion are real but the expected QBI
  benefit is zero; the elected structure is sub-
  optimal vs alternative arrangements (e.g. lower W-2
  salary to stay below phaseout threshold; multi-
  business structure to separate QBI-eligible from SSTB
  activity). Recovery (selective Mechanism E — retained
  S-corp-experienced CPA): for any SSTB context
  approaching the QBI phaseout, retain CPA for case-
  specific QBI + SSTB + W-2-allocation + multi-state
  modeling BEFORE the S-corp election OR before the
  tax year for which the election applies; the IRS
  Pub authority + The Finance Buff voice are starting
  points; the case-specific arithmetic is CPA
  territory.

  `decisions.md` selective referral category on this

The IRS Pub authority + The Finance Buff + Nolo content
remains useful as **procedural floor + asker preparation
BEFORE CPA consultation**, but NOT as substitute for the
retained-CPA case-specific adjudication. For routine
record-keeping / home-office substantiation / travel
substantiation / accountable-plan setup, the IRS Pub
authority + The Finance Buff content can carry the work;
for D4 / D8 / multi-state / SSTB-phaseout / QSBS-personal-
tax case-specific adjudication, retained S-corp-
experienced CPA is the procedural floor.
