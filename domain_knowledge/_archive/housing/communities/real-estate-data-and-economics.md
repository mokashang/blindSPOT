# Real-Estate Data and Economics

The macro-cycle-anchored, data-series voice — Calculated Risk (Bill
McBride), the FHFA House Price Index series, housing-economist
research, and the data-and-trajectory feed that informs Marketplace
and NYT-real-estate-desk explainers. This community is a SOURCE of
months-of-supply trajectory, housing-starts seasonality, MBA
mortgage-application index reading, HPI quarterly releases, and
GSE-policy / conforming-loan-limit signal; it does NOT issue
individual-decision recommendations and its blind spot is its
confidence-without-individual-applicability. Per
`domain_knowledge/housing/blindspots.md` (`high_stakes: false`),
self-directed reading of these sources is the recovery move for
macro-cycle (F14) framing — paired with the transactional voice from
`real-estate-experience-forums` for tactical execution; CPA / real-
estate-attorney consultation is reserved for the dollar-significant
tail (1031, conforming-loan-limit cliff arithmetic at large balances,
GSE-policy interpretation on an in-flight loan).

## Voice

Data series with quarterly cadence, economist-blogger commentary,
and Fed-watching context. Posts are time-series-and-chart ("MBA
purchase-application index down 4.2% w/w, lowest reading since
March 2024; here's the chart"), commentary-anchored ("months-of-
supply ticked up to 4.1 from 3.8; historically a 4–6 month supply
is the soft-balance zone"), and policy-cycle-aware ("the conforming
loan limit for 2026 is $806,500; high-balance counties go higher;
this is the cliff that drives jumbo pricing in May 2026"). Calculated
Risk reads like a one-economist blog that's been compounding for
15+ years — daily-or-weekly data update, the chart, a paragraph of
context, the historical comparison. The FHFA HPI series read like
quarterly bureaucratic press releases with a 12-month lag in
narrative interpretation — the data is authoritative, the framing
is non-existent. Housing-economist voice (Robert Shiller,
Mark Zandi, Diana Olick at CNBC for the explainer-end, NAR's chief
economist for the institutional end which the community treats
adversarially per `framings.md` F13 anti-source posture) reads like
a Brookings paper or a Fed Working Paper depending on the venue;
the unifying voice is "the data says X, the framing-of-the-week
says Y, here is why X is the durable signal."

## Mental model

- Months-of-supply is the load-bearing market-condition metric.
  Below 4 months → seller's market with appreciation pressure;
  4–6 months → soft-balance; above 6 months → buyer's market with
  price-flat-to-decline pressure. The metric integrates inventory
  AND sales velocity in one number; it leads price-trajectory by
  6–12 months historically.
- The MBA mortgage-application index leads existing-home-sales by
  4–6 weeks. Purchase-application volume is the leading edge of
  transaction volume; refinance-application volume is the leading
  edge of refinance activity (and inversely correlated with rate
  trajectory). Both series report weekly with seasonal adjustment;
  reading the year-over-year compare and the 4-week-moving-average
  is the discipline.
- Housing-starts seasonality and trajectory is the supply-side
  signal. Single-family starts lead single-family completions by
  6–8 months and single-family inventory by 9–12 months. The
  multifamily-starts series leads rent-trend signal by 12–24
  months (a 2-year multifamily-starts boom in a metro foreshadows
  rent-softening at the delivery wave).
- FHFA HPI is the authoritative national-and-MSA price series, but
  it lags by ~60 days from the quarter close. Case-Shiller is the
  alternate 20-MSA repeat-sales series; the two diverge in
  methodology details that matter for HCOL coastal-MSA reading
  (FHFA caps the loan size at the conforming limit; Case-Shiller
  weights price-tiers differently). The cycle-aware reader uses
  both.
- The conforming-loan-limit annual reset (announced by FHFA in
  late November for the following year, effective January 1)
  drives the jumbo-vs-conforming pricing cliff. The 2026 limit
  ($806,500 baseline, higher in high-cost counties up to
  $1,209,750) is the cutoff above which loan pricing changes
  materially (LLPA grid, lender overlays, jumbo-spread to
  conforming). Buyers near the limit make different decisions
  knowing the cliff structure.
- The Fed Funds rate does NOT directly set mortgage rates. The
  10-year Treasury yield does (mortgage-MBS spread to the 10-year
  is the actual driver), and the 10-year is set by the bond
  market's expectations about future short-rate trajectory + term
  premium + Fed balance-sheet posture. Conflating "Fed cut rates"
  with "mortgage rates fell" is a recurring misconception the
  community corrects in every cycle.

## Known blind spots OF this community

- **Macro confidence routes individual askers into market-timing
  failures.** The community's data-and-cycle frame is well-
  calibrated at the national-and-MSA level — the months-of-supply
  inflection has historically led price-trajectory; the
  multifamily-delivery wave has historically softened rent at
  the delivery quarter; the MBA application index has historically
  led existing-home-sales. None of this is dispositive on whether
  an individual asker should buy this specific house this specific
  month. Trigger: an asker cites a Calculated Risk post or an FHFA
  HPI trend as the basis for "I'm going to wait until [Q3 2026]"
  on a personal-housing decision. Failure mode: the asker waits
  out of the market, household event (kid hits kindergarten, lease
  ends, parent relocation) forces a forced-move during the wait,
  and the asker transacts under worse circumstances than the
  buy-now-and-hold counterfactual; the macro framing was right on
  trajectory and wrong on this household's binding constraint
  (per `framings.md` Calculated Risk voice anchor blind spot
  bullet). Recovery (self-directed for primary residence;
  professional-mediated for high-balance / late-cycle decisions):
  use macro data as context-input to the holding-horizon
  question — a late-expansion-phase signal raises the bar for
  transaction-cost amortization (per `blindspots.md` F11) and
  argues for either a longer hold or a rent-and-wait posture,
  but does NOT replace the asker's personal-timing constraints.
  Pair Calculated Risk reading with the
  `real-estate-experience-forums` voice on transaction-tactics
  for execution timing; for any cycle-divergent metro with a
  >$1M purchase, consult a fee-only CFP on the opportunity-cost
  arithmetic before locking the timing decision.

- **National-and-MSA framing misses sub-MSA / micro-market
  variance that drives individual decisions.** The community's
  data series resolve at the national level (Calculated Risk
  weekly), the MSA level (Case-Shiller 20-city), and at best the
  Census-tract level (FHFA quarterly). The actual decision is
  property-level — this house, this block, this school-attendance
  boundary, this HOA, this flood-zone. The MSA may be cooling
  while the school-zoned-for-[district] sub-market is still hot;
  the national HPI may be flat while the climate-stressed coastal
  ZIP is repricing materially. Trigger: an asker is using
  national-or-MSA HPI trajectory to anchor expectations for a
  specific neighborhood or property type where the sub-market
  divergence is large. Failure mode: the asker either over-pays
  in a hot sub-market because the macro framing said "the market
  is soft" (it's soft 5 miles north; not on this block), or
  under-bids in a soft sub-market because the macro framing said
  "the market is tight" (it's tight at the median; not at this
  price-tier on this block). Recovery (self-directed): pair
  macro reading with `real-estate-experience-forums` metro-
  subreddit micro-market intelligence (school-zone reputation,
  gentrification trajectory, walkability and transit reality)
  AND with property-tier-specific comps from the local MLS via
  the buyer's agent; do NOT use national or MSA HPI as the
  primary input on price-discovery for a specific offer.

- **Conforming-loan-limit cliff arithmetic is structural and
  invisible to non-specialists.** The community reports the
  conforming-loan-limit reset annually and notes the jumbo-spread
  context, but the cliff structure — that a loan at $807k in 2026
  is jumbo-priced (LLPA + lender overlay + product-availability
  constraints) and a loan at $805k is conforming-priced — has
  per-month-PITI implications on the order of $100–300/mo at the
  current rate spread, and product-availability implications
  (jumbo loans frequently have stricter DTI / reserve / asset
  documentation overlays than conforming loans). Trigger: an asker
  is purchasing near the conforming-loan-limit boundary
  ($760k–$850k range in 2026 baseline counties; higher in high-
  cost counties) and is comparing rate-quotes that may straddle
  the cliff without realizing it. Failure mode: the asker
  underwrites at a quoted rate that assumes conforming-loan
  pricing, then the loan is restructured at higher jumbo pricing
  late in the process (after appraisal lowers the loan amount or
  closing costs push the loan above the cliff), and the household
  PITI is materially higher than budgeted at lock. Recovery
  (mortgage-broker / loan-officer side for product-comparison;
  self-directed pre-screen): cross-reference the current-year
  conforming-loan-limit on FHFA's site against the proposed loan
  amount AND the high-cost-county schedule for the property's
  location; for any loan within $50k of the cliff, get paired
  rate quotes (conforming + jumbo) from at least two lenders
  and ask explicitly which product the quote assumes — the cliff
  awareness is the broker's job but the asker has to surface the
  question, which the macro-data sources don't prompt.

- **Fed-policy translation to mortgage-rate-trajectory is
  consistently mis-modeled by retail readers.** The community is
  sharp on the mortgage-MBS-spread-to-10-year mechanism, but the
  popular-press translation — "Fed cut rates, mortgages will fall"
  — survives every cycle because Marketplace and the
  `journalists-and-explainers` voice flatten the chain for
  accessibility. The retail asker who reads the Fed-cut headline
  often assumes a 25bp Fed cut maps to a 25bp mortgage-rate fall,
  when historically the mortgage market has already priced the
  expected Fed path into the 10-year and mortgage rates can move
  in either direction on the Fed-meeting day depending on dot-plot
  vs market-expectation divergence. Trigger: an asker is timing a
  mortgage-rate-lock or a refinance trigger around a Fed-meeting
  expectation. Failure mode: the asker delays the lock to "let the
  Fed cut," the 10-year rises on the meeting day because the Fed
  was less dovish than priced, the mortgage rate moves UP, the
  asker either takes the higher rate or loses the lock-extension
  window; the Fed-narrative routing was based on a mis-modeled
  causal chain (per `framings.md` F5 / F14 cluster). Recovery
  (self-directed): read Calculated Risk's and Mortgage News
  Daily's (in `mortgage-and-personal-finance`) coverage of the
  mortgage-MBS-spread-to-10-year mechanism explicitly; for any
  in-flight loan lock decision, do not time around the Fed
  meeting — lock when the deal-clock requires, and use the
  refinance-trigger math (break-even months on the refi cost)
  as the durable rate-improvement decision later, not the
  pre-Fed-meeting lock as the rate-timing artifact.

- **Quantitative authority routes attention away from
  consumer-advocacy and tenant-rights framings the data series
  do not measure.** The data series measure transactions,
  inventories, prices, and rates — they do not measure
  appraisal-bias rates, foreclosure-mill operational patterns,
  HOA-reserve-study pathology, eviction-process timelines, or
  rent-stabilization-statute changes. An asker who anchors their
  housing decision in the quantitative-data reading is being
  routed toward the question "what does the market say" and away
  from the question "what does the system do to people in my
  situation." The systemic-friction framings live with
  `journalists-and-explainers` (ProPublica) and
  `consumer-advocacy-and-tenant-rights` (NLIHC / Legal Aid).
  Trigger: an asker is making a renter-side decision (tenant in a
  stabilized unit considering a buyout, tenant facing a
  non-renewal, prospective buyer of a unit in a building with a
  problematic HOA-reserve history) and is consulting the
  macro-data community for the answer. Failure mode: the
  data-and-economics framing returns "the market is X" and routes
  the asker's attention away from the tenant-protection-statute
  or the HOA-reserve-pathology that is the actual binding
  variable on the decision. Recovery (self-directed cross-
  reference; attorney for ambiguous statutory cases): when the
  asker's decision sits in a tenant-rights or consumer-advocacy
  framing (F12 / F13 per `framings.md`), the macro data is
  context input only; the binding reading comes from
  `consumer-advocacy-and-tenant-rights` for tenant-side statute
  and from `journalists-and-explainers` (ProPublica) for HOA
  reserve-study and appraisal-bias data; for jurisdiction-
  specific statutory ambiguity, retain a local real-estate or
  landlord-tenant attorney.
