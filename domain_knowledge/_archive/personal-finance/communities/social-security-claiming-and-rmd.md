## Mental model

Social Security claim-strategy is a *joint-optimization problem
under longevity-uncertainty with permanent-arithmetic
consequences*: the primary-insurance-amount (PIA, the basic
benefit at full retirement age) is calculated from the asker's
35 highest-earning years' indexed earnings (the AIME, average-
indexed-monthly-earnings); the claim-age at which the asker
files determines whether the asker receives reduced benefits
(file before FRA — 67 for those born 1960+), unreduced PIA (file
at FRA), or augmented benefits via *delayed-retirement-credits*
(DRCs, 8%/year between FRA and 70 — the canonical "delay-to-70
buys an 8% real-return guaranteed annuity" framing); the file-
once decision compounds into the *surviving-spouse benefit*
under the higher-earner-survivor rule, which means the higher
earner's claim-age determines the surviving-spouse benefit floor
(and the surviving spouse is typically the longer-lived,
compounding the load-bearing-ness of the higher-earner's
delayed-claiming decision). The Mike Piper Open-Social-Security
framework optimizes *expected present value* of all current and
future benefits under the asker's specified longevity-
distribution (defaults to SSA actuarial life-tables 2020, but
the asker can adjust for health-status); the Larry Kotlikoff
Maximize-My-Social-Security framework optimizes *consumption-
smoothing-utility* across pre- and post-claim years; the two
frameworks produce *different specific recommendations* in some
cases (notably, Kotlikoff's consumption-smoothing framework may
support earlier claiming for some households where PV-
maximization supports later, because the consumption-smoothing
target discounts utility differently than expected-PV-
maximization). The asker should run both optimizers and make
the optimization-target choice explicitly. WEP / GPO mechanics
for non-covered public-pension households (federal CSRS / certain
state-teacher-pension + state-municipal-pension systems that
did *not* pay into Social Security) are a load-bearing complication
the canonical SS-claim-strategy framework engages but the broader
retirement-planning community routinely under-engages: WEP
(Windfall Elimination Provision under §215(a)(7)) reduces the
PIA by a formula based on the non-covered-pension income, and
GPO (Government Pension Offset under §202(k)(5)) reduces or
eliminates spousal / survivor benefits by 2/3 of the non-covered-
pension income — for a public-employee household with significant
non-covered-pension income, the SS-claim-strategy arithmetic
shifts materially. The Social Security Fairness Act 2024 (signed
Jan 5 2025) repealed WEP and GPO entirely, but the implementation
timing (phase-out, retroactive adjustments) is pending Treasury
regulations and the practitioner community is in the middle of
re-running claim-strategy arithmetic for the post-WEP-GPO regime.
RMD-orchestration is the *coordinated companion* problem: the
asker who delays SS to 70 also accumulates higher traditional-
IRA / 401k balance through the bridge years, which forces higher
RMDs at age 73 / 75 (SECURE 2.0 §107 phase) and pushes MAGI into
higher IRMAA / NIIT / ACA-APTC brackets in the post-RMD-age
years. The optimal joint-strategy is to *fill the lower-bracket
years pre-RMD-age with Roth conversions* (drawing down
traditional-IRA at the 12% / 22% / 24% bracket pre-SS + pre-RMD
to reduce the post-RMD-age traditional-IRA balance and the
forced-RMD amount), then *delay SS to 70 for the DRC + survivor-
benefit-floor maximization*, then *use QCD (Qualified Charitable
Distribution) at 70½ to satisfy RMD without MAGI inclusion* (up
to $108k for 2025, indexed), then *manage IRMAA / NIIT brackets
through the RMD years with the smaller post-conversion
traditional-IRA balance*. The inherited-IRA 10-year-window under
SECURE 1.0 final regulations Feb 2024 is the load-bearing
post-2024 trap: for a non-eligible-designated-beneficiary
inheriting from a decedent who had reached RMD age, the final
regulations require *annual* RMDs in years 1-9 of the 10-year
window *plus* full distribution by end of year 10 — not the
previously-assumed-by-many flexible-10-year distribution. This
materially changes the inherited-IRA-receiving asker's
withdrawal-sequencing arithmetic.

## Known blind spots OF this community

- **WEP / GPO repeal implementation timing is uncertain and
  the community is currently re-running claim-strategy
  arithmetic for households where the pre-repeal arithmetic
  has been the operating reference.** The Social Security
  Fairness Act 2024 (signed Jan 5 2025) repealed WEP and GPO
  entirely, but the implementation timing — phase-out,
  retroactive adjustments to currently-claiming
  beneficiaries, treatment of households who claimed before
  the repeal under the pre-repeal arithmetic — is pending
  Treasury regulations as of mid-2026 (the typical Treasury-
  regulation timeline from statute-enactment to final-
  regulation is 12-36 months; the Feb 2024 SECURE 1.0
  inherited-IRA final regulations took 4+ years from the
  Dec 2019 SECURE enactment). The community's current
  practitioner-blog voice is in transition between the
  pre-repeal arithmetic (still operative for currently-
  claiming households until Treasury issues final
  regulations) and the post-repeal arithmetic (operative
  prospectively but uncertain on retroactive scope). Trigger:
  a non-covered-public-pension household reads a Mike Piper
  *Oblivious Investor* post from 2024 walking the WEP / GPO
  arithmetic; the asker reads a 2025 post on the repeal;
  the asker doesn't know which arithmetic to use for their
  own claim-strategy in 2026. Failure mode: asker delays
  claiming on pre-repeal arithmetic that anticipates WEP
  reduction; the repeal phases in mid-claim; the asker's
  delayed-claiming arithmetic was over-conservative.
  Recovery: cross-reference against `personal-finance/
  sources.yaml` source `kotlikoff-and-ssa-statement-
  walkthroughs` for the federally-funded SHIBA / SHIP
  channel (non-commissioned, structurally-aligned with the
  asker's interest) at shiphelp.org for the per-state-
  current-arithmetic counseling; verify the current
  arithmetic against the SSA POMS section governing the
  household's specific claim type before claim-strategy
  finalization; for households at high stakes, retain a
  fee-only Social Security claiming specialist (Joe
  Elsasser CFP / Covisum / Social Security Timing
  practitioner network) for the case-specific arithmetic
  — F7 lifecycle / retirement-decumulation framing in
  framings.md.

- **Joint-optimization across SS + RMD + Medicare + ACA-
  APTC + state-tax is a multi-variable problem the
  practitioner-class fee-only-CFP community routinely
  under-engages relative to its load-bearing-ness.** The
  Open-Social-Security + Maximize-My-Social-Security
  optimizers solve the *SS claim-strategy* sub-problem
  conditional on assumed-other-income; the RMD-orchestration
  + Roth-conversion-laddering + IRMAA-bracket-management
  + NIIT-management + ACA-APTC-pre-65-management + state-
  tax-on-Roth-conversion sub-problems require a *separate
  set of tools* (Kitces *Roth conversion sizing* + Pfau
  *bond tent and rising-equity-glide-path* + CPA
  marginal-rate-arithmetic) that the broader fee-only-CFP
  community routinely deploys *sequentially* rather than
  *jointly*, missing the cross-variable optimization that
  shifts the optimal claim-age + RMD-orchestration
  strategy. The Mike Piper *Oblivious Investor* framing
  explicitly bridges these (the *Social Security Made
  Simple* book and the *Taxes Made Simple* book overlap on
  the joint-optimization problem), but the SS-claim-
  strategy specialist community is small relative to the
  broader fee-only-CFP community, and the asker routing to
  a generic-retirement-planning fee-only CFP may get the
  SS-claim-strategy sub-problem solved well without the
  RMD-orchestration + IRMAA + ACA-APTC sub-problems solved
  at all. Trigger: an asker retains a NAPFA-registered fee-
  only CFP without explicit SS-claim-strategy + RMD-
  orchestration practice depth; the CFP runs Open-Social-
  Security for the claim-age decision but doesn't run the
  Kitces Roth-conversion-sizing arithmetic for the bridge-
  year traditional-IRA drawdown that would reduce the
  post-RMD-age MAGI exposure. Failure mode: asker delays
  SS to 70 on PV-maximization grounds, accumulates higher
  traditional-IRA balance through bridge years, faces
  higher RMD at 73 + higher IRMAA bracket + higher NIIT
  exposure in the post-RMD years — the SS-claim-strategy
  was locally optimal but the joint SS + RMD-orchestration
  was sub-optimal relative to the available joint-
  optimization. Recovery: require explicit fee-only-CFP
  practice-depth verification on the joint SS + RMD-
  orchestration problem (the practitioner should be
  running *both* Open-Social-Security or Maximize-My-Social-
  Security *and* Roth-conversion-sizing + IRMAA-bracket-
  management arithmetic in the same engagement); cross-
  reference against `personal-finance/sources.yaml`
  communities `fee-only-fiduciary-cfp` (for the Pfau RICP
  retirement-income-certification depth signal) and `tax-
  and-cpa-practitioner` (for the marginal-rate + IRMAA-
  arithmetic + Form 8606 basis-history reconciliation
  depth signal) — F7 lifecycle / retirement-decumulation
  framing in framings.md.

- **The Open-Social-Security vs Maximize-My-Social-
  Security optimization-target distinction is consequential
  but the practitioner community routinely deploys one tool
  without naming the other.** Mike Piper's Open-Social-
  Security optimizes *expected present value* under the
  asker's specified longevity-distribution + discount-rate;
  Larry Kotlikoff's Maximize-My-Social-Security optimizes
  *consumption-smoothing utility* across pre- and post-
  claim years; the two frameworks produce *different
  specific recommendations* in some cases (notably, the
  consumption-smoothing framework may support earlier
  claiming for some households where PV-maximization
  supports later, because consumption-smoothing discounts
  utility differently than expected-PV). The practitioner-
  community-blog voice in fee-only-CFP and retirement-
  economics-academics routinely deploys Open-Social-
  Security recommendations without naming the consumption-
  smoothing alternative, and the asker who runs Open-SS
  only does not see the framework-choice variable. Trigger:
  a Mike Piper *Oblivious Investor* post or a Kitces
  *Nerd's Eye View* post deploys the Open-SS recommendation
  for a representative case; the asker treats the
  recommendation as the answer without engaging the
  framework-choice. Failure mode: asker delays SS to 70
  on Open-SS PV-maximization recommendation; the household
  consumption-trajectory in the 62-70 bridge years is
  materially constrained (because the asker is drawing
  down portfolio while delaying SS); the asker dies at 71;
  the consumption-smoothing utility-loss in the bridge
  years exceeds the PV-maximization gain that never
  materialized; under Kotlikoff's consumption-smoothing
  framework the optimal claim age would have been 65 with
  better intertemporal-utility outcome. Recovery: require
  *both* Open-Social-Security and Maximize-My-Social-
  Security runs against the asker's actual SSA earnings-
  record + marital-status + longevity-expectation; the
  household makes the framework-choice (PV-maximization vs
  consumption-smoothing) explicitly based on intertemporal-
  preferences, not implicitly through tool-selection;
  cross-reference against `personal-finance/sources.yaml`
  community `retirement-economics-academics` (the
  Kotlikoff lifecycle-finance academic framework + the
  Pfau-Kitces PV-maximization framework as the academic-
  literature anchor for the framework-choice variable) —
  F7 lifecycle / retirement-decumulation framing in
  framings.md.

- **State-specific public-pension WEP / GPO arithmetic
  is uneven across states and the canonical SS-claim-
  strategy community routinely defers to "consult your
  state public-employee pension publications" without
  providing a state-by-state arithmetic protocol.** WEP
  / GPO mechanics depend on the *specific non-covered-
  pension system* the asker participates in — federal
  CSRS pensions vs FERS hybrid (FERS replaced CSRS in
  1987 for new federal hires; current federal employees
  may be in CSRS, FERS, or CSRS-Offset depending on
  hire-date) is one axis; state-teacher-retirement-system
  (TRS) participation varies by state (some states are
  Social Security-covered, others are not; CA, IL, KY,
  LA, ME, MA, MO, NV, OH, TX have significant non-covered
  state-teacher populations; others — like NY's TRS — are
  Social Security-covered with no WEP / GPO exposure);
  municipal firefighter / police pensions vary by
  jurisdiction; railroad retirement (the Railroad
  Retirement Board administers RRB instead of Social
  Security for railroad workers, with very different
  benefit calculation rules). The canonical SS-claim-
  strategy community engages WEP / GPO at the federal-
  rule level but routinely defers state-specific
  detail. Trigger: a CA-state-teacher household asks
  Open-Social-Security or Maximize-My-Social-Security
  for the claim-strategy arithmetic; the tool engages
  WEP / GPO at the federal level but does not engage
  CA-State-Teachers'-Retirement-System (CalSTRS)-
  specific pension-formula interactions that the
  asker's actual benefit calculation requires. Failure
  mode: asker uses the federally-arithmetic
  recommendation without state-specific pension-formula
  reconciliation; the actual benefit-amount on claim is
  materially different from the optimizer's prediction.
  Recovery: route the state-specific arithmetic to the
  state-public-employee-pension-system's own member-
  services + the federally-funded SHIBA / SHIP volunteer
  for the state-specific Medicare-and-SS-coordination
  layer; cross-reference against the *American Federation
  of Teachers* or the *National Education Association*
