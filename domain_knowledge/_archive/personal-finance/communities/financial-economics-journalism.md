critique of personal-finance industry incentives), behavioral-

literature produces in narrative form), and the macro-context
(Wessel at Brookings on the retirement-savings-system structural
critique and the SS / Medicare trust-fund-depletion arithmetic
that frames the policy-volatility-framing); it is **not a
substitute** for case-specific advice from a retained fee-only-
fiduciary CFP, retirement-experienced CPA, or state-bar attorney
on the asker's specific situation. The Zweig column, the
Tergesen Roth-conversion piece, the Wessel Hutchins-Center
analysis, or the NPR *Planet Money* episode tells you where the
analysis lives in narrative form; the retained named professional
with case-specific facts is where the binding determination
occurs.

personal-finance-influencer / mass-media voice (Suze Orman, Dave
Ramsey, Ramit Sethi) in that the journalism community is bound
by editorial-standards on factual accuracy and structural-
policy-context, while the influencer voice is monetization-
optimized and routinely simplifies past the point of edge-case
accuracy.

## Mental model

Household financial decisions are shaped by *structural-policy-
context + industry-incentive-architecture + behavioral-finance
realities* that the academic / practitioner-blog framing
under-engages, and the journalism layer's role is to *name the
specific structural-context and incentive-architecture that
makes the abstract recommendation hard or wrong in practice*.
The policy-volatility framing is load-bearing: tax-law sunsets
(TCJA 2017 sunset Dec 31 2025 reverting individual marginal
brackets to pre-TCJA rates and reducing standard deduction by
roughly half; ACA-APTC enhanced subsidies under ARPA / IRA
expiring Dec 31 2025; estate-tax-exemption $13.99M / $27.98M
MAB sunset Dec 31 2025; SECURE 2.0 §603 Roth-only catch-up
requirement for W-2 wages >$145k starting 2026 after IRS
Notice 2023-62 administrative-relief delay; SECURE 2.0 §107
RMD age phase from 73 → 75 over 2033; SECURE 1.0 inherited-
IRA 10-year-window final regulations Feb 2024), Medicare
solvency arithmetic (Part A trust fund depletion projection
2031-2036 range with progressive revisions), Social Security
solvency arithmetic (OASDI trust fund depletion projection
2033-2035 with 75-79% benefit payable from current revenue
post-depletion absent Congressional action) — the academic-
voice optimization frameworks assume *current law* as the
load-bearing variable; the journalism layer's job is to
remind the asker that *current law sunsets* and the planning
decision must explicitly account for the post-sunset
scenario, not just the pre-sunset arithmetic. The advisor-
fraud + industry-incentive-architecture framing is the
second load-bearing analytical axis: Zweig's *Intelligent
Investor* column archive catalogs the recurring patterns of
advisor misconduct (variable-annuity-rollover into IRA at
high commission with no documented suitability analysis;
Madoff / Stanford / Allen-Stanford fraud-as-discoverable-
through-DD scenarios; bank-affiliated broker-dealer
self-dealing on proprietary funds; the 401k-rollover-to-IRA
recommendation that benefits the advisor's AUM more than the
client's outcome under DOL Fiduciary-Rule / SEC Reg-BI
scrutiny); Olen's *Pound Foolish* critique catalogs the
structural-incentive failures in the personal-finance
industry (commission-driven product sales as default
delivery channel; "free seminar" + variable-annuity-pitch
predatory-marketing patterns; financial-illiteracy as a
revenue-extracting feature of the industry); Tergesen's WSJ
retirement-coverage routinely surfaces the gap between
DOL / SEC regulatory aspirations and on-the-ground
implementation. The behavioral-finance realism axis
translates the academic-research-literature (Kahneman /
Tversky / Thaler / Mullainathan-Shafir / Benartzi) into
narrative form that names the *systematic gap between
optimization-frame-recommendation and revealed-preference-
behavior* — the asker who *should* execute the Roth-
conversion-ladder per the technical framework but
*doesn't* because of mental-accounting + loss-aversion +
short-term-thinking is the typical case, not the edge case.

## Known blind spots OF this community

- **Journalism narrative-grounding does not give actionable
  case-specific recommendations and the asker who treats the
  column as actionable receives non-actionable framing.** The
  journalism layer is *editorially constrained* against
  giving individual advice — Tergesen's WSJ Roth-conversion
  piece tells the asker *what Roth conversion is and what
  policy variables affect it*, but does not run the
  arithmetic on the asker's specific Form 1040 marginal
  rate. The mental-model gap is that the asker who reads
  the column may *expect* an actionable recommendation
  the journalism is structurally not delivering, and the
  asker may *substitute* the journalism's narrative-
  context for the retained-CPA / fee-only-CFP arithmetic
  that the case actually requires. Trigger: an asker reads
  a Tergesen Roth-conversion piece, decides to "do a Roth
  conversion this year" based on the narrative-context,
  but doesn't run the marginal-rate arithmetic against
  IRMAA / NIIT / ACA-APTC binding constraints with a
  retained CPA. Failure mode: the conversion-amount
  pushes MAGI past the IRMAA 2-year-lookback bracket, the
  Part B + Part D premium surcharge at age 65 increases
  by $300+/month for the following 2 years, and the asker
  did not size that into the conversion-arithmetic.
  Recovery: route the conversion-arithmetic to a retained
  retirement-experienced CPA (community `tax-and-cpa-
  practitioner`) plus a retirement-income-certified fee-
  only CFP (community `fee-only-fiduciary-cfp`) for the
  IRMAA + NIIT + ACA-APTC cliff arithmetic before
  executing — F11 IRS-publication-authority + F2
  marginal-rate-vs-policy-volatility framing in framings.md.

- **The journalism cycle's *what's-new-this-week* cadence
  systematically under-weights the *durable-structural-
  truth* that doesn't change week-to-week.** Personal-
  finance journalism is news-cycle-driven; the front-of-
  WSJ-Markets-section coverage tracks Fed-meeting +
  inflation-print + earnings-season + policy-headline
  cadence, and the durable-structural-truths (3-fund-
  portfolio dominates active management over 10+ years;
  saving 15-25% of income from age 25 is sufficient for
  most retirement scenarios; tax-loss harvesting under
  the wash-sale rule is bounded by the substantially-
  identical-security rule; the IRS-Pub-authority anchor
  must be cross-checked against practitioner-blog
  interpretation; the fee-only fiduciary structure is the
  structural-conflict-of-interest-mitigation that the
  CFP credential alone does not provide) get re-derived
  intermittently in evergreen-feature pieces but do not
  dominate the daily / weekly coverage. The asker who
  reads the journalism continuously gets a *news-cycle-
  filtered* view of personal-finance that emphasizes
  recent-policy-events at the expense of durable-
  structural-truths. Trigger: a week of WSJ + NYT
  personal-finance coverage focuses on a single Fed
  meeting + a single Treasury Direct policy change + a
  single SEC enforcement-action; the asker reading
  continuously emphasizes the news-cycle-events in
  their own decision-making and under-weights the
  durable-truth (priority-order, asset-allocation,
  saving-rate) that the news-cycle is not covering.
  Failure mode: asker makes tactical decisions on the
  news-cycle-noise (selling-into-a-Fed-meeting
  panic, buying-on-a-policy-rumor) that lose to the
  durable-truth-of-stay-the-course discipline.
  Recovery: cross-reference against `personal-finance/
  sources.yaml` community `bogleheads-and-passive-index`
  for the durable-truth anchor (the Bogleheads
  *Investment Priority Order* + *Three-Fund Portfolio* +
  *Stay the Course* discipline is the structural-truth
  the journalism cycle systematically under-engages) and
  against `retirement-economics-academics` for the
  multi-decade lifecycle frame — F11 behavioral-finance
  framing in framings.md.

- **The journalism layer's editorial standards on the
  fraud / misconduct beat protect against false-positives
  in advisor accusations but produce false-negatives where
  the misconduct pattern hasn't yet been litigated or
  publicly disclosed.** Zweig's *Intelligent Investor*
  archive on advisor misconduct, Tergesen's WSJ
  retirement-coverage on fiduciary-rule-vs-suitability-
  standard gaps, and ProPublica's IRS / wealth-
  inequality investigative archive all operate under
  editorial-standards that require (a) documented
  evidence, (b) right-of-reply to the accused, (c) legal
  review before publication — which protects against
  false-positives but produces a structural lag between
  the misconduct happening and the journalism reporting
  it. The asker who relies on the journalism layer for
  advisor-vetting may *miss* a misconduct pattern at an
  advisor who hasn't yet been publicly named. Trigger:
  an asker reads Zweig's archive on variable-annuity-
  rollover misconduct, retains an AUM-fee advisor at a
  firm not yet named in Zweig's coverage, the firm has
  ongoing-but-not-yet-disclosed rollover-recommendation
  patterns that match the Zweig archive. Failure mode:
  asker's 401k rolls into IRA at the AUM firm, asker is
  recommended into a variable-annuity inside the
  rollover, the variable-annuity-commission arithmetic
  benefits the advisor more than the asker, the
  arrangement is later litigated under DOL Retirement
  Security Rule but the asker's funds were misallocated
  for years before the litigation arrived. Recovery:
  cross-reference against `personal-finance/sources.
  yaml` community `erisa-and-fiduciary-rule-law` (the
  ERISA-practitioner-attorney voice + SEC IAPD
  adviserinfo.sec.gov Form ADV Part 2 Brochure + FINRA
  BrokerCheck disclosure-record-reading procedure) for
  the systematic advisor-vetting protocol — F14
  policy-risk + advisor-fraud framing in framings.md.

- **The journalism layer's macro-policy framing (Wessel,
  CBPP, TPC) is structurally biased toward the
  policy-economist-DC-establishment perspective and
  under-engages the household-implementation reality.**
  The Brookings *Hutchins Center* / *Center on Budget
  and Policy Priorities* / *Tax Policy Center* /
  *Center for American Progress* coverage on retirement-
  savings-system structural critique, SS / Medicare
  trust-fund-depletion arithmetic, and tax-distributional
  analysis is *expert-grounded* and *macro-coherent*
  but routinely under-engages the *household-
  implementation reality* — what the typical asker does
  when the policy shifts, what the typical asker
  understands vs misunderstands about the policy, what
  the typical asker's revealed preferences are when the
  policy-economist-recommended optimization is presented.
  Trigger: a Wessel Hutchins-Center piece on the
  retirement-savings-system structural critique argues
  for auto-IRA + auto-401k + Save More Tomorrow
  defaults; the asker treats the macro-policy framing
  as binding on individual decision without engaging
  that the asker's actual employer doesn't offer the
  recommended structures and the individual-decision-
  space is what's available not what's policy-
  optimal. Failure mode: asker reads the macro-policy
  critique, treats the personal-finance situation as
  policy-failed-me-and-I-have-no-options, and
  disengages from the individual-decision-space
  optimization that *is* available within current law.
  Recovery: cross-reference against `personal-finance/
  sources.yaml` community `bogleheads-and-passive-index`
  for the individual-decision-space optimization
  (within-current-law priority-order + asset-allocation
  + tax-efficient-fund-placement) and against
  `personal-finance-influencer / mass-media voice` for
  the behavioral-systems framing (Ramit Sethi automated-
  savings systems, Dave Ramsey baby-steps emergency-
  fund-first) that operates within current law without
  waiting for policy reform — per framings.md F11
  behavioral-finance framing.

- **The journalism layer's coverage of fee-only-vs-fee-
  based advisor structure is shallower than the practitioner-
  community-blog voice and routinely defers to "consult
  a financial advisor" without the structural-distinction-
  protocol.** WSJ, NYT, and the mainstream personal-finance-
  journalism layer engage the fiduciary-vs-suitability-
  standard distinction in evergreen-feature pieces but
  routinely close columns with "consult a financial
  advisor" without the protocol-distinction that the
  fee-only-fiduciary-CFP community-blog voice provides
  (NAPFA / XY-Planning-Network / Garrett-Planning-
  Network registry + Form ADV Part 2 Brochure + Part 3
  Form CRS verification + SEC IAPD + FINRA BrokerCheck).
  The asker who acts on "consult a financial advisor"
  without the protocol-distinction defaults to the
  bank-affiliated-broker-dealer or the
  insurance-affiliated-fee-and-commission-hybrid advisor
  who is the *most-marketed* but not the most-aligned-
  to-asker-outcome. Trigger: an asker reads a Tergesen
  Roth-conversion piece that closes with "consult a
  financial advisor"; the asker walks into the
  bank-affiliated advisor's office for the Roth-
  conversion consultation; the advisor's compensation
  is AUM-fee on the eventual rollover plus commission
  on any variable-annuity sold into the IRA. Failure
  mode: the Roth-conversion-recommendation the
  bank-affiliated advisor gives is biased toward
  *converting more* (to grow the eventual AUM base)
  and toward *rolling-out-of-401k-into-IRA* (to
  capture the AUM fee), which may not be the
  arithmetically-correct recommendation for the asker.
  Recovery: cross-reference against `personal-finance/
  sources.yaml` community `fee-only-fiduciary-cfp`
  (the NAPFA / XY-PN / Garrett registry +
  fee-only-vs-fee-based-distinction protocol) and
  community `erisa-and-fiduciary-rule-law` (the SEC
  IAPD + FINRA BrokerCheck verification procedure)
  before any consultation — F10 professional-referral
  framing in framings.md.

## Mechanism E posture

Personal-finance is `high_stakes: true` per
`domain_knowledge/_meta_ontology.md` §5 and the canonical
Mechanism E gating case named in `docs/specs/ROADMAP.md` §5.
Every blindspot above ends with a Recovery move routed to a
named professional channel. For financial-economics-
journalism-sourced framings the default channel — when the
question crosses from policy-context-narrative into binding
household decision — is *not* a single named professional
class but a *structured routing* to the appropriate channel
for the specific decision: **withdrawal-sequencing / Roth-
conversion-laddering / IRMAA-bracket-management** decisions
route to a retained retirement-experienced CPA (community
`tax-and-cpa-practitioner`) plus a retirement-income-
certified fee-only CFP (community `fee-only-fiduciary-cfp`);
**SS-claim-age + survivor-benefit sequencing** decisions
route to a fee-only Social Security claiming specialist
(community `social-security-claiming-and-rmd`); **estate-
tax-exemption-sunset planning + trust formation** decisions
route to a state-bar-admitted estate attorney with
high-net-worth-estate-tax practice depth (ACTEC fellowship
credential signal); **401k-rollover / fiduciary-breach /
DOL-Retirement-Security-Rule scrutiny** decisions route to
a retained ERISA attorney (community `erisa-and-fiduciary-
rule-law`); **ACA-APTC enrollment + subsidy-arithmetic**
decisions route to a CMS-trained Navigator (per
`domain_knowledge/health-insurance/sources.yaml` community
`aca-marketplace-and-navigator`); **Medicare-coordination**
decisions route to a state SHIBA / SHIP volunteer (per
`domain_knowledge/health-insurance/sources.yaml` community
`medicare-shiba-and-government`). For credential verification
on any individual recommended, to **SEC IAPD
adviserinfo.sec.gov** for Form ADV Part 2 Brochure + Part 3
Form CRS, and **FINRA BrokerCheck brokercheck.finra.org**
for disciplinary-disclosure record. This community is the
policy-context-narrative + advisor-fraud-archive +
behavioral-realism translation layer; the retained named
professional with case-specific facts is the binding-
determination layer. The journalism community itself is
structurally non-advice-giving, which is its strength on
the narrative-grounding side and its constraint on the
case-specific-arithmetic side.
