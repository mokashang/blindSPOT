# 一亩三分地 (1point3acres)

Mandarin-language community of Chinese-international SDE applicants,
new-grads, and mid-career engineers based in (or routing through)
the US. Founded around 2009 as a graduate-school admissions board,
now the dominant CN-language forum for US tech job-hunting, visa
mechanics, and family-located life-in-US decisions. The community
self-organizes by board (offer board, lottery board, layoff board,
H-4 board, 海归 board) and rewards detailed first-person narrative
("我的 timeline", "我是怎么 negotiate 的", "裁员的 60 天我做了什么")
over abstracted advice.

## Voice

Code-switching Mandarin-English. Visa-mechanic terms stay English
(`H1B`, `OPT`, `STEM`, `cap-gap`, `AC21`, `PERM`, `I-140`); social
and emotional content stays Chinese (`焦虑`, `劝退`, `劝进`, `稳`,
`水`, `老板`, `家里`). Detail-rich and timeline-oriented: posts
typically lead with status timeline ("F1 → OPT → STEM → H1B Year 2,
PERM pending"), location, comp anchor (`base + RSU + sign-on`),
and the actual question. Self-deprecating about non-tech career
prestige; aggressive about FAANG / 大厂 / unicorn comp benchmarking;
suspicious of generic English-language advice ("律师都这么说" — what
attorneys say generically is treated as a starting point, not the
answer). Pseudonymous accounts; reputation is built on accuracy of
prior timelines.

## Mental model

- **Visa status is the binding constraint.** Until I-485 is filed
  (or, conservatively, approved), every career decision is filtered
  through "does this lose me status?" The default risk weight on a
  status lapse is higher than the comp delta of any reasonable job
  switch — a $30k base difference can be erased by a 12-month
  out-of-status episode.
- **The pipeline is deterministic if you don't break it.** F-1 →
  OPT → (STEM-OPT) → H-1B-lottery → AC21-portability → I-140 →
  AOS → green card. Each transition has a filing window; the
  community knows the windows by heart and treats deviation as the
  costly failure mode.
- **大厂 sponsorship history is the load-bearing signal.** Whether
  an employer has historically sponsored H-1B / PERM / EB-2 for
  CN-nationals is more predictive than recruiter promises. The
  community maintains informal lists; FAANG and a small tier of
  unicorns are the default-trusted set.
- **Family-located decisions cascade fast.** H-4 spouse, parent
  B-2 visiting, kids' state-residency-for-school — each couples
  to the principal's status timeline. The 家庭 sub-threads treat
  this as a planning surface, not an afterthought.
- **The community is its own oracle.** Generic LLM / attorney-blog
  framings are calibrated against 1p3a anecdata; if 50 timeline
  posts say AC21 §106(c) same-or-similar adjudication has tightened
  this year, that is the operative reading regardless of what
  Murthy wrote in 2021.

## Typical concerns

- **Lottery odds and lottery hedging** — multi-employer registration
  ethics, day-1-CPT MS pipeline as a fallback (and its USCIS-scrutiny
  exposure), F-1 re-enrollment to restart the clock.
- **Sponsorship-willingness asymmetry in offer comparison** — a
  $190k base with sponsorship commitment beats $220k base without,
  by the community's revealed-preference ordering.
- **AC21 §106(c) "same or similar"** — when a mid-PERM employer
  switch preserves the priority date and when it does not.
- **60-day grace clock under layoff** — does the severance period
  count, what cosmetic-employment / unpaid-leave structures do or
  don't extend the clock, when to file H-1B transfer vs change of
  status to B-2 vs depart.
- **H-4-EAD vulnerability** — what an H-4-EAD-rescission rule would
  do to the household's two-income assumption; whether spouse should
  pursue an independent H-1B / O-1.
- **Return-to-China timing under EB-2 China backlog** — the GC
  is 8-10 years out under the current bulletin; at what point does
  the ROI of waiting flip negative?

## Known blind spots OF this community

- **Visa-security maximization is the default and crowds out
  career-capital framing.** 1p3a's revealed preference is sponsorship
  certainty over employer quality and skill-trajectory; a Bay-Area
  mentor would say the asker is leaving 10-year-career-capital value
  on the table by filtering for a B-tier sponsor-willing employer
  when a top-tier employer would sponsor on a one-month delay.
  Recovery: surface F2 (Long-term career capital) framing when the
  asker is over-weighting near-term status certainty.

- **Modal-asker bias toward FAANG / 大厂.** The community's most-
  upvoted timelines are FAANG-track. Non-FAANG paths (research labs,
  Series-B engineering teams, infrastructure-startup founding
  engineers) are systematically under-represented even when they
  match the asker's skill profile and visa profile better. A new-grad
  with a Series-B offer and a FAANG offer reads 1p3a and concludes
  FAANG is the dominant choice when the Series-B may be equity-rich
  and equally sponsorship-willing.

- **Visa-mechanic claims drift faster than the threads do.** USCIS
  policy updates (e.g. the February 2023 CSPA "sought to acquire"
  shift, beneficiary-centric H-1B registration) take months to
  propagate through 1p3a's high-traffic threads. A 2021-2022 thread
  cited as authoritative may be operating on a superseded reading.
  Recovery: cross-check any visa-mechanic timing claim against the
  current USCIS Policy Manual or an attorney-blog dated within the
  last 6 months.

- **Return-to-China discussions skew toward people who already
  left.** 海归 threads on 1p3a often surface from returnees, not
  from people still deciding. The framing systematically under-
  represents the in-place-on-H-1B asker's lived calculus
  (visa-extension cycles, family-located arc, geopolitical posture
  swings). Cross-route to `china-returnee-voices` for the
  multi-year-retrospective view.

- **English-language adversarial / contrarian framings are
  filtered out.** Matt-Levine-school IPO-mechanic counter-narratives,
  Patrick-McKenzie offer-negotiation counter-narratives, and
  immigration-attorney "consult counsel" framings show up
  pre-translated through 1p3a's own filter. The community absorbs
  what it agrees with and drops the rest. Recovery: when the
  decision involves IPO mechanics, offer negotiation against a
  reluctant employer, or self-petition route choice (EB-1A / NIW),
  route to F2 / F3 / F6 framings explicitly to get the
  counter-narrative back.
